# Mizoram–Myanmar Border Relations

**Tags:** #geography #politics #diaspora

Mizoram shares a **510 km international border with Myanmar (Burma)**, the longest India-Myanmar border of any Indian state.

## The Ethnic Connection
The border is a colonial-era political line that bisects the ethnic Zo/Mizo homeland. The Chin people of Myanmar's Chin State are ethnically, linguistically, and culturally identical to the Mizo people of India — separated only by a surveyor's line drawn by the British in 1891.

## Post-2021 Refugee Crisis
Following the **February 2021 military coup in Myanmar**, tens of thousands of **Chin refugees** crossed into Mizoram. The Mizo people responded with massive, spontaneous community support — housing, feeding, and sheltering refugees, drawing deeply on the [[Tlawmngaihna]] spirit of unconditional hospitality to fellow Zo people.

The state government under [[Zoramthanga]] formally welcomed the refugees and refused central government pressure to deport them, citing the ethnic kinship obligation.
