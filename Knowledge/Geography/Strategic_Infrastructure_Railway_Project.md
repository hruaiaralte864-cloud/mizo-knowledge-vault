# Bairabi-Sairang Rail Link Project

**Tags:** #geography #infrastructure #connectivity

The **Bairabi-Sairang Rail Link** is a 51.38 km broad gauge railway project that will connect the state of Mizoram to the national rail network of India. It is a critical component of the "Act East" policy.

## Technical Specifications
| Feature | Detail |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Total Length** | 51.38 km |
| **Bridges** | 158 Major/Minor Bridges |
| **Tunnels** | 23 Tunnels (Totaling 12.6 km) |
| **Main Station** | Sairang (Near Aizawl) |
| **Project Cost** | ~₹8,000 Crore |

## Engineering Marvel: Bridge No. 196
Located near Sairang, **Bridge 196** is one of the tallest railway bridges in India. 
- **Height:** 156 meters (Taller than the Qutub Minar).
- **Construction:** Uses high-strength pier systems to withstand seismic activity in the Mizo hills.

## Economic Impact
Once completed, the rail link will reduce the cost of transporting essentials like food grains and fuel by **30-40%**, significantly lowering the cost of living in **[[Aizawl_District]]**.
