# Phawngpui — The Blue Mountain

**Tags:** #geography #nature #landmark

**Phawngpui** (*Phawng* = dome-shaped, *pui* = great/large) is the highest peak in Mizoram at **2,157 metres (7,077 ft)** above sea level. Located in the Lawngtlai district near the Myanmar border, it dominates the southern Mizo landscape.

## Mythology
In Mizo legend, Phawngpui is considered the **abode of the gods** — specifically the dwelling place of celestial beings and the Lasi (forest fairies). Its summit is frequently draped in deep blue mist, which gives it the name "Blue Mountain."

## Biodiversity
The **Phawngpui National Park** surrounding the peak protects extraordinary biodiversity:
- Rare orchid species including the [[State_Symbols|Senhri (Red Vanda)]].
- The endangered **[[State_Symbols|Vavu (Mrs. Hume's Pheasant)]]**.
- Leopards, Serow ([[State_Symbols|Saza]]), and Hoolock gibbons.
