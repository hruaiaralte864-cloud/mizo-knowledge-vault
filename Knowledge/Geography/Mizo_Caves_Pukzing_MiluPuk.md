# Mizo Caves: Repositories of Ancient Shadows



Mizoram's ridges contain some of the most legend-rich caves (*Puk*) in the Northeast, serving as historical sanctuaries and archaeological sites.

## Pukzing Puk: The Largest Cave
Located in Pukzing village near Marpara, **Pukzing Puk** is the state's largest cave. Legend says it was carved by the strongman **[[Mualzavata]]** using only a hairpin. Scientifically, it is a significant limestone-shale cavity with various chambers that were historically used as grain storage during inter-tribal wars.

## Milu Puk: The Cave of Skulls
Situated near **Zote** village, **Milu Puk** (Cave of Skulls) was discovered to contain hundreds of human skulls. Archaeological data suggests these were non-Mizo peoples (possibly pre-Lusei tribes) who were trapped or buried there during ancient migrations. It is a critical site for the **[[Ancient_Migrations_Mizo_Origins]]** research.

## Lamsial Puk: The Battle Remembrance
Located near **Vaphai** village, **Lamsial Puk** is a memorial cave containing skeletal remains from a specific pre-colonial inter-tribal battle. It serves as a stark reminder of the "Ridge Wars" era before the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]** stability.
