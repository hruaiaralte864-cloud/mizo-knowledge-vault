# Kawrtethawveng Village

**Tags:** #geography #village #border_trade

**Kawrtethawveng** is a significant village in the Mamit district, located on the western border of Mizoram. It is a crucial point of contact between the Mizo hills and the plains of Tripura and Bangladesh.

## Cross-Border Dynamics
Because of its location, Kawrtethawveng has historically been a center for informal trade and cultural exchange. It has a diverse population that includes Mizo and Reang (Bru) communities. The village played a prominent role during the resettlement of Bru families, serving as a point of negotiation and community building.

## Infrastructure and Development
The village is home to a major police checkpost and is a key node for the **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** digital literacy initiatives. The local economy is driven by the production of broom grass and bamboo, which are transported to the plains for processing.

## Cultural Importance
Kawrtethawveng is famous for its "border markets" where local produce is bartered for salt, clothes, and tools from the plains. This has fostered a unique "frontier culture" that blends hill traditions with trade pragmatism.
