# Khawkawn Village



**Khawkawn** is a historically significant village located in the northeastern part of Mizoram. It is known for its scenic beauty and its strategic position near the Tiau River, which forms the international border with Myanmar.

## History and Significance
The village is often mentioned in **[[Mizo_Migration]]** oral histories as one of the transit points for clans moving from the east into the central Lushai Hills. It has preserved many traditional customs that have vanished in more urbanized centers like Aizawl.

## Economy and Lifeways
The primary occupation of the residents is **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]**, but in recent years, there has been a significant shift towards horticultural crops, especially ginger and passion fruit. The village is also known for producing high-quality **[[Mizo_Bamboo_Baskets]]** and agricultural tools.

## Tourism Potential
Due to its proximity to the border and the "Vengpui" (Great View) cliffs, Khawkawn is becoming a destination for eco-tourists and historians. It offers a glimpse into the authentic Mizo rural life (*Thinglang Nun*).
