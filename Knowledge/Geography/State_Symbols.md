# Mizoram State Symbols



## State Animal — Saza (Serow)
*Capricornis sumatraensis* — a goat-antelope with shaggy black and grey fur, adapted to the steep, rocky cliffs of the Mizo hills. The Saza is nimble, shy, and fiercely territorial — qualities the Mizo admire.

## State Bird — Vavu (Mrs. Hume's Pheasant)
*Syrmaticus humiae* — a spectacularly beautiful long-tailed pheasant found in bamboo-forested hillsides at mid-elevations. Critically endangered; [[Phawngpui_Blue_Mountain|Phawngpui National Park]] is one of its last refuges.

## State Flower — Senhri (Red Vanda)
*Vanda tessellata* (or the Red Vanda orchid) — a brilliantly red-orange orchid that grows as an epiphyte in the forest canopy. Features prominently in Mizo poetry and [[Rokunga|Rokunga's]] songs.

## State Tree — Herhse (Ironwood)
*Mesua ferrea* — known for its extremely dense, durable hardwood and fragrant blossoms. Once common in Mizo forest; now increasingly threatened by [[Jhum_Cultivation|Jhum agriculture]] and logging.
