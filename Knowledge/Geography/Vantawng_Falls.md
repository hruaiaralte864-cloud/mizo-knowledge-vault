# Vantawng Falls



**Vantawng Falls** is the highest waterfall in Mizoram and the tallest waterfall in Northeast India, with a vertical drop of approximately **750 feet (229 metres)**.

Located in the **Serchhip district**, the falls cascade from a dense jungle ridge down into a deep, mist-filled gorge. The name derives from *Vawng* (a type of jungle creeper) and refers to the creeper-covered cliffs surrounding the falls.

## Ecology
The spray zone at the base of the falls sustains a permanently humid micro-ecosystem hosting:
- Rare pteridophytes (ferns) and mosses.
- Orchid species including the state flower *[[State_Symbols|Senhri]]*.
- Endemic insect species.

The falls are accessible via a steep forest trail from Thenzawl town and are a growing ecotourism destination.
