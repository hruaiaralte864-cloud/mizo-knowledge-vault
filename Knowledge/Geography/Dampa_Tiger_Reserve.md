# Dampa Tiger Reserve

**Tags:** #geography #nature #wildlife

**Dampa Tiger Reserve** (established 1994) is Mizoram's only Tiger Reserve and the state's largest protected area, covering **500 sq km** in the Mamit district in northwest Mizoram along the Bangladesh border.

## Wildlife
The reserve is a critical wildlife corridor in the India-Bangladesh transboundary ecosystem. Known species include:
- **Royal Bengal Tiger** (critically low numbers, rarely seen but GPS-collar tracking confirmed presence).
- **Clouded Leopard** — one of the few confirmed habitats in Northeast India.
- **Hoolock Gibbon** — the only ape species in India.
- Asian Elephant, Gaur (Indian Bison), Sambar Deer.

## Ecological Significance
The dense semi-evergreen forests of Dampa are remnants of the original Mizo hill ecosystem before [[Jhum_Cultivation|Jhum farming]] cleared much of the landscape.
