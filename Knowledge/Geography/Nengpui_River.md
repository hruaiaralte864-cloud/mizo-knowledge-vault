# Nengpui River

**Tags:** #geography #nature #waterways

The **Nengpui River** is an important waterway in southern Mizoram, primarily flowing through the Lawngtlai and Saiha districts. It is a vital resource for the local Chakma and Mizo communities living along its banks.

## Hydrology and Course
The Nengpui is known for its clear waters and its course through dense tropical semi-evergreen forests. It eventually joins the larger **[[Chhimtuipui_River]]** system, which flows into the Bay of Bengal through Bangladesh.

## Biodiversity
The river basin is a hotspot for aquatic life and migratory birds. It is also home to the **[[Nengpui_Wildlife_Sanctuary]]**, which protects rare species such as the Clouded Leopard and various endemic orchids. The river provides water for diverse **[[Flora_of_Mizoram]]** including bamboo and teak.

## Local Economy
Villages along the Nengpui rely on the river for fishing and as a transportation route during the monsoon season when road connectivity can be difficult. The fertile silt deposited during floods supports **[[Rice_Cultivation_Mizoram]]** in the valley areas.
