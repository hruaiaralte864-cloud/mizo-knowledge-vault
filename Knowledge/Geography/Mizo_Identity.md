# Mizo Identity — Zomi and Pan-Zo Consciousness



The term **Mizo** literally means "hill people" (*Mi* = people, *Zo* = hill/cold). However, identity consciousness goes deeper — many Mizo people identify with the broader **Zomi** or **Zo** ethnic grouping, which encompasses the wider Kuki-Chin peoples across:

- Northeast India (Mizoram, Manipur, Assam)
- Myanmar (Chin State, Sagaing Region)
- Bangladesh (Chittagong Hill Tracts)

## The Question of Greater Mizoram
The [[Mizo_National_Front]] originally sought a united *Greater Mizoram* incorporating all Zo-speaking peoples across these territories — a demand quietly set aside in the [[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]] in exchange for statehood.

## Modern Identity
Today, Mizo identity is a confident, joyful synthesis of:
- **Pre-Christian heritage:** Tlawmngaihna ethics, Jhum agriculture, oral tradition.
- **Christian faith:** Nearly 90% Protestant, structuring daily life.
- **Modern India:** Education, sports, music, and technology integration.
- **Pan-Zo solidarity:** Strong cross-border kinship consciousness with Chin and Kuki peoples.
