# Lengpui Airport: Table-Top Engineering



**[[Lengpui_Airport]]** is unique as India's first large airport to be built in the hills of the Northeast on a "Table-Top" runway.

## Engineering the Plateau
Located in the **[[Mamit_District]]**, the airport's runway was engineered by flattening a ridge and filling a valley. This "Cut and Fill" method is technically challenging because the soil in Mizoram is prone to high-moisture erosion. 

## Hydrological Management
The airport is surrounded by several mountain streams (**Tlangsang**). Managing the runoff from the heavy Mizo monsoon is critical to preventing "Runway Subsidence." The airport uses complex subterranean drainage networks, a subject of study for the **[[Aizawl_Smart_City_ITMS]]** drainage teams.

## Operational Importance
It is the primary lifeline for the **[[Mizoram_Tourism_Connectivity]]** and the **[[Act_East_Policy_Strategic_Vision]]**, connecting Aizawl to Kolkata, Guwahati, and Silchar. Its elevation and topographical constraints require specialized pilot training.
