# Lungsen (West) Village



**Lungsen (West)** is a strategically located village in the Lunglei district, near the western border of Mizoram. Its proximity to the Bangladesh border makes it a significant point for cross-border trade and cultural exchange.

## Demographic Mix
Unlike the central Mizo villages, Lungsen has a diverse population that includes Mizo, Chakma, and Bru communities. This mix is reflected in the local dialect and the variety of **[[Mizo_Handicrafts_Textiles]]** produced in the area.

## Connectivity and Development
The village serves as a hub for the western Lunglei region. It is connected to the main district headquarters via the state highway, though terrain-related challenges often impact travel during the monsoon. The **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** has a presence here, supporting local farmers and small traders.

## Nature and Environment
The surrounding hills are covered in dense bamboo forests, which provide raw materials for the local **[[Ebony_Knife_Handles_Craft]]** and construction.
