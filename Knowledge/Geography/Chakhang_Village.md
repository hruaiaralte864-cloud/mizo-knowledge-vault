# Chakhang Village



**Chakhang** is one of the most remote and high-altitude villages in the Saiha district, located within the **[[Mara_Autonomous_District_Council_MADC]]**. It is a primary settlement of the Mara people.

## Historical Isolation and Preservation
Due to its remote location near the Indo-Myanmar border, Chakhang has preserved many Mara customs that have been diluted elsewhere. It is known for the maintenance of traditional Mara architectural styles in its older buildings and the continues use of the Mara language in its purest form.

## Nature and Wildlife
The forests surrounding Chakhang are part of the broader **[[Palak_Wildlife_Sanctuary]]** ecosystem. The area is home to rare avian species and the Hoolock Gibbon. The village is often a starting point for trekkers seeking to explore the unexplored peaks of the southern hills.

## Economy
The villagers practice a specialized form of **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]** adapted for high altitudes. They are also known for their skill in traditional weaving, producing the distinctive Mara shawls characterized by intricate geometric patterns.
