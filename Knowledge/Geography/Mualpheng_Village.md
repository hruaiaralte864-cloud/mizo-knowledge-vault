# Mualpheng Village



**Mualpheng** is a traditional Mizo village located in the Aizawl district. It is known for its high elevation and the preservation of ancient stone heritage sites.

## Historical Landmark: Mualpheng Monoliths
The village is home to several ancient vertical monoliths (**[[Lungphun]]**) that were erected by early Mizo settlers. These stones often mark the sites of ancient feasts or memorialize great warriors. The engravings on these stones are a subject of study for historians interested in pre-colonial **[[Mizo_History_Overview]]**.

## Community Life
Life in Mualpheng centers around the village square and the local church. The community is deeply committed to **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**, especially during the agricultural harvest seasons. The village also maintains a traditional **[[Zawlbuk]]** (though mostly symbolic today), representing the historical Mizo bachelors' dormitory.

## Economy
The villagers primarily practice **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]**, focusing on rice and legumes. In recent years, vanilla and organic ginger have become important cash crops for the local economy.
