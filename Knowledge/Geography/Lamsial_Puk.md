# Lamsial Puk (The Cave of Lamsial)



**Lamsial Puk** is a historically and archaeologically significant cave located near the village of Farkawn in the Champhai district. It is one of the most famous caves in Mizoram, known for the "skeleton mystery" that intrigued historians for decades.

## The Discovery of Skeletons
Inside the cave, hundreds of human skeletons were discovered stacked neatly. Local legend suggests that these were the victims of a great tribal war between the villages of Lamsial and Farkawn in the distant past. Scientific analysis indicates that the skeletons date back to the pre-Christian era of **[[Mizo_History_Overview]]**.

## Cultural Significance
The cave is a key site for the **[[Cave_Conservation_Society_Mizoram]]**. It is considered a hallowed ground and is a major stop on the heritage circuit of Champhai. It provides evidence of the ancient Mizo practice of collective burial or memorialization in caves before the adoption of modern **[[Mizo_Death_Rites_Thane]]**.

## Tourism and Conservation
Lamsial Puk is easily accessible via a short trek from Farkawn. Efforts are underway to preserve the site as a Protected Monument of Mizoram to ensure the ancient remains are not disturbed by visitors.
