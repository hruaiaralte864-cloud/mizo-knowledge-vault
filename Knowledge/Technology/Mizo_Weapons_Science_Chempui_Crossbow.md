# Mizo Weapons Science: Metallurgy and Physics



Mizo weaponry evolved specifically for guerrilla warfare in dense bamboo jungles, prioritizing reliability and local materials.

## The Chempui (Mizo Machete)
The **Chempui** is the most versatile Mizo tool/weapon. Traditionally forged from carbon-steel (often traded from Burmese or Bengali markets), its weight is concentrated toward the tip to allow for powerful "Cleaving" strikes through thick bamboo. The metallurgy focuses on a "Hard Edge, Soft Spine" logic, similar to katanas, to prevent shattering during impact.

## The Kha-ti (Crossbow)
The **Mizo Crossbow** is a masterpiece of bamboo engineering.
- **The Bow**: Made from aged *Raw-ngal* (sturdy bamboo) for maximum elastic tension.
- **The Trigger**: A simple but effective toggle mechanism carved from buffalo bone or hardwood.
- **The Arrows**: Tipped with poison (derived from local vines) or hardened by fire.
The physics of the Kha-ti allowed for silent, long-range kills in the dense canopy, a major challenge for the **[[Lushai_Brigade_WWII]]** scouts.
