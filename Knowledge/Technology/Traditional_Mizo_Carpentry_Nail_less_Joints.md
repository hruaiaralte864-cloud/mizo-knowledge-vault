# Traditional Mizo Carpentry: Nail-less Engineering



Ancient Mizo architecture, particularly the **[[Zawlbuk_Mizo_Bachelors_Dormitory]]** and the Chief's house, was built without a single metal nail.

## The Logic of Joints
Mizo carpenters used "Mortise and Tenon" joints and "Lap Joints" secured with cane bindings (*Hruih*).
- **Seismic Flexibility**: This "Elastic Connection" system was crucial. During the frequent **[[Seismology_of_Mizoram_Ridges]]** events, the nail-less houses would sway and distribute the stress rather than snap.
- **Tooling**: Most work was done using a single **[[Mizo_Weapons_Science_Chempui_Crossbow]]** (Chempui), showcasing an extreme efficiency of tool-to-task.
