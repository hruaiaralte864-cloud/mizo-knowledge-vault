# Drone Logistics: "Medicine from the Sky"

**Tags:** #tech #health #logistics #drones

Mizoram is a pioneer in India for using heavy-lift drones to overcome the extreme logistical challenges of the "Vertical Ridge" terrain.

## The Problem: The "Last Mile" Hill Challenge
In districts like **[[Siaha_District]]** or **[[Mamit_District]]**, a 20-kilometer road journey can take several hours due to landslides or winding paths. This delay is life-threatening for medical diagnostics or specialized vaccines.

## The Solution: Aerial Corridors
Under the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]**, the state government launched the "Medicine from the Sky" project. Automated drones are used to:
- Deliver vaccines and emergency blood bags from **[[Aizawl_District]]** to remote rural health centers.
- Transport blood samples for rapid diagnostic testing back to centralized labs.
- Monitor forest health and mapping under the **[[Green_Mizoram_Campaign_Governance]]**.

## Regulatory Leadership
Mizoram was among the first states to map "Red and Green" drone zones across its airspace, creating a safe digital corridor for log-tech startups modeled on the **[[Startup_Ecosystem_Aizawl]]**.
