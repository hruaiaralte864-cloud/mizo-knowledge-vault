# Land Ports of Mizoram: Zokhawthar and Zorinpui

**Tags:** #economy #trade #geopolitics #act_east

Mizoram's land ports are the strategic valves of India’s **"Act East Policy,"** facilitating legitimate cross-border trade with Myanmar and Bangladesh.

## 1. Zokhawthar (Indo-Myanmar Border)
Located in the **[[Champhai_District]]**, Zokhawthar is the most active border trade point in the state. It handles the export of agricultural produce and the import of consumer goods. Under the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**, the infrastructure here is being upgraded to a "Modern Land Port" with integrated customs and cold-storage facilities for products like **[[Mizo_Plum_Concentrates_Economy]]**.

## 2. Zorinpui (Indo-Bangladesh Border)
Zorinpui in the **[[Lawngtlai_District]]** is the terminal point of the **[[Kaladan_Multi-Modal_Transit_Transport_Project]]**. Once fully operational, it will connect Mizoram to the Chittagong port in Bangladesh. This port is expected to revitalize the southern economy, creating a "Transit Corridor" that links the **[[Lai_Autonomous_District_Council_LADC]]** directly to global maritime trade.

## Economic Security
The **[[Mizoram_Police_Commandos]]** and central security forces maintain a high level of vigilance at these ports to prevent the smuggling of narcotics while facilitating the movement of the **[[Zohnahthlak]]** people who live on both sides of the border. These ports are the primary assets for the **[[Mizoram_2050_Projections]]**.
