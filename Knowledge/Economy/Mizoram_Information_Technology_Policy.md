# Mizoram Information Technology (IT) Policy



The **Mizoram IT Policy** provides the regulatory and fiscal framework for transforming the state into a "Digital Hill State," leveraging its high literacy rate for service-sector growth.

## Strategic Objectives
- **IT-Enabled Services (ITeS)**: Attracting BPOs and KPOs to Aizawl by providing high-speed connectivity and power subsidies. 
- **E-Governance**: Total digitization of the **[[Governance_Overview]]**, specifically the **[[Land_Revenue_Settlement_Mizo]]** and the **[[Mizoram_Loka_SEWA]]** portals.
- **Cyber Security**: Under the **[[Mizoram_Cyber_Security_Initiative]]**, the state is building a "CERT-Mizoram" to protect the digital assets of the **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** and other critical infrastructure.

## Human Capital Development
A major pillar of the policy is the "Digital Literacy Drive" coordinated by the **[[Mizoram_Youth_Commission_MYC]]**. It aims to train 10,000 youth annually in advanced coding, AI, and cybersecurity. This is the workforce that will power the **[[Startup_Ecosystem_Aizawl]]**.

## Infrastructure: The IT Park
The state is developing a dedicated "IT Park" near the **[[Lengpui_Airport]]** to provide plug-and-play infrastructure for international tech firms. This is envisioned as the core energy behind the **[[Mizoram_2050_Projections]]** for a knowledge-based economy.
