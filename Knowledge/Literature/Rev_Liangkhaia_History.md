# Rev. Liangkhaia and the First History

**Tags:** #literature #history #biography

**Rev. Liangkhaia** (1884–1979) was a pioneering Mizo historian, poet, and church leader. He is most famous for writing the first systematic history of the Mizo people, titled **'Mizo Chanchin'** (1938).

## The Historian's Task
Before Liangkhaia, Mizo history existed almost entirely as oral tradition, songs, and legends. He took on the monumental task of interviewing elders across the hills, cross-referencing clan lineages, and reconciling mythological origins like [[Sinlung_Origin_Myth]] with historical migration patterns.

## Literary Style
His prose was clear, authoritative, and deeply respectful of the Mizo past. He was the first to give the Mizo people a sense of a unified, documented history that could stand alongside the histories of other nations.

## Impact
His work laid the foundation for all subsequent Mizo historical studies. Every student of Mizo history today begins with the groundwork laid by Liangkhaia. He was also a prolific composer of hymns that are still sung in churches today.
