# Mizo Poetry Eras



Mizo poetry has evolved through three distinct eras, each reflecting the changing social and spiritual landscape of the people.

## 1. The Pre-Literate / Classical Era
Composed primarily of oral songs (**[[Hlado_And_Music|Hlado]]**), war chants, and elegies. These were characterized by rigid rhythmic structures and intense focus on nature, warfare, and tribal glory.

## 2. The Revivalist / Hymnal Era (1900–1950)
Following the arrival of missionaries, the focus shifted to Christian themes. This era produced the great hymns of [[Kaphleia]] and others. The language became more reflective and spiritual.

## 3. The Modern Era (1950–Present)
Initiated by poets like [[Rokunga]], modern Mizo poetry expanded into themes of nationalism, social commentary, and romantic longing for the land. Today, Mizo poetry is experimental and vibrant, blending traditional metaphors with modern global styles.
