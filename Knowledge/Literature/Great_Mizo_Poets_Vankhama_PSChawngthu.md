# The Great Mizo Poets: Vankhama and P.S. Chawngthu

**Tags:** #literature #poetry #biography #culture

Mizo literature transition from oral tradition to written excellence is defined by two giants: **Vankhama** and **P.S. Chawngthu**.

## Vankhama: The Nature Poet
Known as the "Wordsworth of the hills," **Vankhama**'s poetry captured the raw, spiritual beauty of the Lushai ridges. His work is characterized by deep "Onomatopoeia"—mimicking the sounds of streams and mountain winds. He is a primary figure in the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters]]** archives.

## P.S. Chawngthu: The Patriotic Soul
P.S. Chawngthu’s work defined the Mizo identity during the 20th-century transition. His poems often dealt with the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]** sentiment and the longing for unity among the **[[Zohnahthlak]]** clans. His songs are still sung as unofficial national anthems in Aizawl.

## The "Lianchhiari" Legacy
Both poets were heavily influenced by the legendary tragic figure **[[Lianchhiari_Poetry_Translation_Attempt]]**, whose ancient lyrics provided the "Metrical DNA" for modern Mizo verse. Their legacy is preserved in the **[[Mizo_Musical_Archives]]**.
