# James Dokhuma (1932–2007)



**James Dokhuma** was a legendary Mizo novelist, historian, and social activist. He is widely regarded as the most important novelist in the Mizo language and was awarded the **Padma Shri** for his literary contributions.

## Master of the Novel
Dokhuma published over 30 books, including historical novels like **'Thaduna leh Lianzuali'** and several works on Mizo customary law and history. His writing was known for its deep immersion in the old Mizo world — he used a rich, classical vocabulary that preserved many dying terms.

## Political Life
Beyond his writing, James Dokhuma was a prominent member of the [[Mizo_National_Front]] during the insurgency years. His experiences during this period, including time spent in prison, influenced his later writing, which often dealt with themes of identity, struggle, and justice.

## Legacy
He is remembered as a "man of the soil" who lived simply but wielded massive intellectual influence. The **James Dokhuma Memorial College** in Aizawl is named in his honour.
