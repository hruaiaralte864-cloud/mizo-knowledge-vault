# Mizo Dialect Attrition: Biate, Hrangkhawl, and Mualthuam

**Tags:** #language #linguistics #culture #biodiversity

While **Duhlian** is the standard Mizo language, several minor dialects within the **[[Zohnahthlak]]** umbrella are facing attrition.

## The Biate and Hrangkhawl Dialects
The **Biate** and **Hrangkhawl** speakers are primarily located in the border areas of **[[Siaha_District]]** and neighboring states. These dialects contain "Lost Vocabulary" regarding ancient mountain spirits and hunting techniques that have vanished from standard Mizo.

## The Standardization Shift
The rapid education movement and the influence of the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters]]** have favored Duhlian for the sake of national unity, causing younger generations to shift away from their clan-specific dialects.

## Preservation Efforts
Modern efforts, including digitized dictionaries and community-led vocal archives, are attempting to map these "Invisible Languages" before they merge into the standard Mizo tongue.
