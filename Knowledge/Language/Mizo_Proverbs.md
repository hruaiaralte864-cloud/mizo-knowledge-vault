# Mizo Proverbs and Idioms

**Tags:** #language #culture #wisdom

The Mizo oral tradition is extraordinarily rich in proverbs (*Thu te leh Hla*, often just *Thu*), which encode centuries of philosophical wisdom, practical survival knowledge, and social ethics.

## Notable Proverbs

> **"Tlawmngaihna hi a kim lo a nih chuan, a zing daih tawk a ni."**
> *If you cannot fully practice Tlawmngaihna, then even dawn is too late.*
> (Meaning: moral duty admits no delays or half-measures.)

> **"Inbuanah chuang lo, inbanlah chuang lo."**
> *One who has not wrestled (Inbuan) has not fully lived.*
> (Meaning: struggle builds character.)

> **"Khaw chi paih chuang lo chuan khua hi a kalh theih lo."**
> *Without a rooster, darkness cannot be chased away.*
> (Meaning: every community needs its brave, vocal initiators.)

> **"Chihhlan hi vak vak a, tihhlana hi thli thlir a ni."**
> *Boasting is a leaf in the wind; true achievement is the mountain.*

## Significance
Mizo proverbs are used in everyday speech by all ages, interwoven naturally into conversations. Elder Mizo people are expected to have an extensive repertoire of proverbs relevant to any situation.
