# The Mizo Language



**Mizo** (also called *Lushai* in older British sources) belongs to the **Tibeto-Burman language family**, specifically the **Kuki-Chin branch**. It is closely related to the languages of the Chin people of Myanmar.

## Classification
- **Family:** Sino-Tibetan → Tibeto-Burman → Kuki-Chin-Naga → Kuki-Chin → Central Kuki-Chin
- **Speakers:** Approximately 800,000 native speakers; understood by nearly the entire population of Mizoram.
- **Script:** Roman alphabet (created 1894 by [[Rev_JH_Lorrain|Lorrain and Savidge]]).

## Linguistic Features
- **Tonal elements:** Not strictly tonal like Chinese, but has significant pitch variation that changes meaning.
- **Verb-final:** Sentences follow Subject-Object-Verb order.
- **Classifiers:** A rich system of noun classifiers.
- **Rich oral tradition:** Huge idiomatic vocabulary for natural phenomena, emotional states, and social relationships with no easy English equivalents (*[[Tlawmngaihna]]*, *Lunglen*, *Hnamte*).
