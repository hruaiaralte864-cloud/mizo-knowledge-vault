# Mizo Dialects and Related Languages



While *Mizo Lusei* (the speech of the dominant Lusei clan) is the standardised official language, Mizoram is linguistically diverse, with several distinct related languages spoken by tribal sub-groups.

## The Dialect Spectrum

**Hmar**
Spoken by approximately 65,000 people in northern Mizoram and southern Manipur. Mutually intelligible with Mizo Lusei to a significant degree. Has its own rich literary tradition and the Hmar people maintain a distinct cultural identity.

**Mara** (Lakher)
Spoken in Siaha District. A Kuki-Chin language but sufficiently distinct from Mizo to be nearly mutually unintelligible. The Mara people have their own script and literary tradition.

**Lai** (Pawi)
Spoken in parts of Lawngtlai District. Very distinct from Lusei Mizo.

**Biate**
A Kuki-Chin language spoken by a small community in Mamit and parts of Assam. Linguistically transitional.

## The Lingua Franca
Mizo Lusei functions as the lingua franca across all these communities and all business, government, and church proceedings in the state operate in Mizo Lusei.
