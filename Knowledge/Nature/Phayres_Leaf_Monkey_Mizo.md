# Phayre's Leaf Monkey: The Spectacled Langur



The **Phayre's Leaf Monkey** (*Trachypithecus phayrei*), also known locally as the "Spectacled Langur" due to its distinct white eye-patches, is an endangered primate found in the deep valleys of Mizoram.

## Habitat and Fragmentation
Primarily found in the **[[Dampa_Tiger_Reserve]]** and the southern **[[Lawngtlai_District]]** forests, these monkeys are highly sensitive to habitat loss. Unlike the adaptable macaques, the Leaf Monkey requires old-growth forests with specific tall tree species to survive.

## Survival Threats
The primary threat is the expansion of **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]** and timber felling, which isolates groups into small "islands" of forest. This fragmentation leads to inbreeding and population decline. The **[[Environment_Forests_and_Climate_Change_Department_of_Mizoram]]** is implementing "Buffer Zone" protections to mitigate this.

## Scientific Research
Mizoram is a "Hotspot" for primate research. Scientists from **[[Mizoram_University_MZU]]** are using GPS tracking and genetic analysis to study the migration patterns of the Leaf Monkey. Protecting this primate is a pillar of the **[[Mizoram_2050_Projections]]** for ecological sustainability.
