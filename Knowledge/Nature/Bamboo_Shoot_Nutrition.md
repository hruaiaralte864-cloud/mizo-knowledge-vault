# Bamboo Shoots (Rawtu): Nutritional Profile



Bamboo shoots, known in Mizo as **Rawtu** (processed) or **Mau** (fresh), have been a nutritional cornerstone of the Mizo diet for millennia. Recent studies have highlighted their emergence as a "superfood."

## Nutritional Components
- **Fiber:** Extremely high in dietary fiber, aiding in metabolic health and weight management.
- **Minerals:** Rich in Potassium and Phosphorus, essential for heart health.
- **Antioxidants:** Contains phenolic compounds that help reduce inflammation.

## Healthcare Utility
In traditional Mizo medicine, bamboo shoot soup was often prescribed for respiratory ailments and to "cool" the body. Modern nutritionists in the **[[Mizoram_State_Commission_for_Women]]** health initiatives promote its consumption as a low-cost, high-nutrient food for rural children to combat malnutrition.

## Commercial Opportunity
Under the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**, the government is promoting the canning and export of bamboo shoots. The **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** offers loans for self-help groups (SHGs) to set up hygienic processing units in the **[[Lunglei_District]]**.
