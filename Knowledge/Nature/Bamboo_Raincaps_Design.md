# Bamboo Raincaps (Lukhum): Design and Utility



The **Lukhum**, or traditional Mizo raincap, is a masterpiece of functional design, crafted from finely split bamboo and the broad leaves of the *Hnahthial* plant.

## Architectural Structure
The Lukhum is designed with a high, peaked crown to ensure that heavy mountain rain runs off immediately. The inner lining is made of waterproof leaves, while the outer shell is a complex lattice of woven bamboo. Its design is inherently aerodynamic, allowing it to stay on the head even during the fierce spring winds (*Tiau-thli*) of the **[[Mamit_District]]**.

## Regional Variations
While the basic design is uniform across the state, subtle variations in the weaving pattern can indicate the wearer's district or clan. For instance, weavers in the **[[Lawngtlai_District]]** often use a tighter weave to withstand the more intense humidity of the southern hills.

## Modern Conservation
Today, while plastic umbrellas have become ubiquitous, the Lukhum remains the preferred choice for farmers practicing **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]** because it leaves the hands free for work. The **[[Mizo_Society_of_Fine_Arts]]** has recently begun promoting the Lukhum as a decorative art piece to ensure that the skill of "raincap weaving" is not lost.
