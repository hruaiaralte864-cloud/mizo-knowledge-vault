# Mizoram Wildlife Taxonomy: Endangered and Endemic Data

**Tags:** #nature #wildlife #ecology #IUCN #biodiversity #research

This node archives high-resolution scientific data on the most critical wildlife species in Mizoram, focusing on their **IUCN Red List Status** and endemic distribution markers.

## 2. Endemic Bird Area (EBA) Markers
Mizoram is part of the **Eastern Himalayas EBA**. Research data indicates that the **[[Phawngpui_Blue_Mountain]]** and **[[Lengteng_Wildlife_Sanctuary]]** act as "High-Altitude Islands" for species found nowhere else in South Asia.
- **Vavu (Blyth's Tragopan)**: Research indicates a specific reliance on the **[[Rhododendron_Altitudes]]** above 1,800m for nesting.
- **Chin Hills Woodpecker**: Observed primarily in the southern districts, marking a critical transition zone between Indian and Southeast Asian avifauna.

## 3. The "Tiger Mystery" Data (Dampa TR)
Dampa Tiger Reserve is famous for having the **highest clouded leopard density** in the world, while its Bengal Tiger population remains elusive.
- **Camera Trap Data**: Over 10,000 hours of footage analyzed (2020-2025) confirms the presence of **Marbled Cats** and **Golden Cats**, but zero confirmed tiger sightings since 2018, leading to a research shift toward "Apex Predator Replacement" theories.

## 4. Primary Threats to Biodiversity
Scholarly research identifies three primary vectors of wildlife decline in the region:
1. **Habitat Fragmentation**: The shift from communal land to private titled lands (under modern agrarian policies).
2. **The "Empty Forest Syndrome"**: A research term for forests that look healthy but lack large mammals due to historical subsistence hunting.
3. **Invasive Species**: The spread of *Lantana camara* in lower elevations of **[[Siaha_District]]** displacing native forage.
