# Flora of Mizoram

**Tags:** #nature #botany #environment

Mizoram is classified as a **biodiversity hotspot** within the Indo-Burma region — one of the planet's 36 most ecologically critical zones. Its forests are among the most species-rich temperate-to-tropical transitional ecosystems in Asia.

## Forest Types
- **Semi-Evergreen Tropical Forests** (below 900m): Dense, multilayered canopy dominated by oak, teak relatives, and wild fig species.
- **Pine Forests** (900m–1,500m): Large tracts of *Pinus kesiya* (Khasi Pine) dominate the mid-elevation ridges. These regenerate rapidly after Jhum cultivation.
- **Temperate Broadleaf Forest** (above 1,500m): Found only on peaks like [[Phawngpui_Blue_Mountain|Phawngpui]], featuring rhododendrons, magnolias, and rare high-altitude orchids.
- **Bamboo Forests:** Covering over 3,000 sq km — see [[Bamboo_and_Crafts]].

## Notable Species
- **[[State_Symbols|Senhri]] (Red Vanda):** The state flower orchid.
- **Wild Orchids:** Over 350 recorded orchid species — among the highest concentrations in India.
- **Wild Banana:** *Musa velutina* and others — ancestors of cultivated bananas.
