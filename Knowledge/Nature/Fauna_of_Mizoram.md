# Fauna of Mizoram

**Tags:** #nature #wildlife #environment

Mizoram's wildlife is extraordinarily rich due to its position as an ecological bridge between the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia.

## Mammals
- **[[Dampa_Tiger_Reserve|Royal Bengal Tiger]]** — critically endangered, in Dampa Reserve.
- **Clouded Leopard** — confirmed in multiple forest areas.
- **Hoolock Gibbon** — only ape species in India; significant populations in Mizoram's north.
- **[[State_Symbols|Saza (Serow)]]** — the state animal, a goat-antelope on cliff faces.
- **Gaur (Indian Bison)** — the largest bovine in the world; hunted traditionally for [[Thangchhuah]] status.
- **Asiatic Wild Dog (Dhole)** — reclusive pack predator.

## Birds
- **[[State_Symbols|Vavu (Mrs. Hume's Pheasant)]]** — critically endangered long-tailed pheasant.
- **Blyth's Tragopan** — incredibly beautiful spectacularly coloured pheasant found at high elevations.
- **Green Peafowl** — found in southern Mizoram near Myanmar border.
- Over **500 bird species** recorded statewide.

## Reptiles
- King Cobra — apex reptilian predator in Mizo forests.
- Reticulated Python — found in lowland forest areas.

## Mammalian Biodiversity Statistics (2017)
Research by H. Lalthanzara (Pachhunga University College) identifies:
- **Total Mammal Species**: 126
- **Families**: 32 families under 11 orders.
- **Large Carnivores**: 3 ursids, 2 canids, 8 felids (including the Marbled Cat).
- **Primate Density**: 8 species (including the Phayre's Leaf Monkey).
- **Rodents & Bats**: 30 rodents and 35 chiropterans (forming 28% of the species).
