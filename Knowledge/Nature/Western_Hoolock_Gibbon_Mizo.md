# Western Hoolock Gibbon: The Singing Ape of the Hills

**Tags:** #nature #biodiversity #wildlife #conservation

The **Western Hoolock Gibbon** (*Hoolock hoolock*) is the only ape species found in India. Mizoram's dense forest canopies are one of its last, most critical strongholds.

## Biology and "The Song"
Hoolock Gibbons are known for their spectacular "duet calls" echoing through the ridges at dawn. In Mizo culture, these calls are seen as the "Spirit of the Forest." They are strictly canopy dwellers, rarely coming to the ground, which makes them highly vulnerable to "Forest Fragmentation."

## Conservation in Dampa
The **[[Dampa_Tiger_Reserve]]** in the **[[Mamit_District]]** is a primary habitat. Conservation efforts here focus on maintaining "Canopy Corridors" to allow families to migrate between ridges. The **[[Mizoram_State_Pollution_Control_Board]]** and local NGOs like *Green Mizoram* work to prevent the clearing of fig trees, the gibbon's primary food source.

## Cultural Status
The Gibbon is a symbol of "Untouched Mizoram." Protecting it is seen as a duty under the **[[Green_Mizoram_Campaign_Governance]]**. Its survival is a key metric for the success of the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]**'s environmental goals.
