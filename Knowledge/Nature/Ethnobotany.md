# Mizo Ethnobotany & Traditional Medicine

Ethnobotany in the Mizo context refers to the complex relationship between the Mizo people and the native flora of the Indo-Burma biodiversity hotspot. For centuries, Mizo *Hriselna* (health) was maintained through deep forest knowledge.

## 🌿 Key Medicinal Plants

### 1. Aihre (Turmeric - Curcuma longa)
- **Traditional Use**: Used as an antiseptic for wounds and skin infections.
- **Academic Context**: Contains curcumin, analyzed for anti-inflammatory properties in modern pharmacology.

### 2. Hnimnuam (Artemisia nilagirica)
- **Traditional Use**: Crushed leaves used for treating stomach aches and as a natural insect repellent.
- **Cultural Value**: Frequent presence in Mizo kitchen gardens.

### 3. Khanghu (Acacia pennata)
- **Traditional Use**: Bark used for traditional hair washing and scalp health.
- **Ecological Role**: Essential for soil nitrogen fixation in *Jhum* fallows.

## 🧪 The Role of the "Tihun" (Traditional Healer)
Historically, the village *Tihun* acted as both a pharmacist and a spiritual guide, understanding exactly which moon phase was best for harvesting specific barks and roots.

## 🏔️ Modern Preservation
Current research at Mizoram University is digitizing these "oral pharmacopeias" to prevent biopiracy and ensure the intellectual property of Mizo indigenous knowledge is protected.
