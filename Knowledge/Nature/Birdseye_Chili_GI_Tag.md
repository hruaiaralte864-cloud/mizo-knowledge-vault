# Mizo Bird's Eye Chilli: The GI Tag Significance

**Tags:** #nature #agriculture #economy

The **Mizo Bird's Eye Chilli**, locally known as **Mizo Chay**, was granted the **Geographical Indication (GI) Tag** in 2015. This certification has transformed it from a kitchen staple into a high-value global commodity.

## Technical Properties
- **Pungency:** Known for its intense heat, measuring between **50,000 to 100,000 Scoville Heat Units (SHU)**.
- **Physical Characteristics:** Small, tapered fruits that grow pointing upwards. They turn from deep green to a vivid, translucent red when ripe.
- **Phytochemicals:** High levels of Capsaicinoids, which are increasingly sought after by pharmaceutical companies for pain-relief balms.

## Economic Impact
The GI tag ensures that only chillies grown in Mizoram can be marketed under this name. This has provided legal protection to local farmers against imitation products. Under the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**, the state has established dedicated "Chilli Processing Zones" to facilitate export to **Dubai, USA, and South Korea**.

## Role in Modern Schemes
The cultivation of Mizo Chay is a primary livelihood avenue under the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** (Handholding Scheme). The **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** provides specialized micro-credit for farmers to transition from wild-harvesting to organic, commercial cultivation.
