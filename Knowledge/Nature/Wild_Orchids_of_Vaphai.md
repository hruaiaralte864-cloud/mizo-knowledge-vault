# Wild Orchids of Vaphai (Champhai District)



The region around **Vaphai village** in Champhai district is world-renowned for its incredible diversity of wild orchids. Due to its unique micro-climate and altitude, it serves as a natural sanctuary for several rare and endemic species.

## Key Species
Researchers have identified over 50 species of orchids in the Vaphai forest tracts, including several varieties of **Dendrobium** and **Bulbophyllum**. One notable orchid is the *Vanda coerulea* (Blue Vanda), which is legally protected due to its rare status.

## Conservation Efforts
The local community, often supported by the **[[Orchidary_Project_Vaphai]]**, has taken significant steps to prevent illegal "bio-piracy" and forest fires that threaten these habitats. The area is a key part of the **[[Flora_of_Mizoram]]** conservation roadmap.

## Significance
The orchids are not just ecologically important but also culturally significant, often mentioned in Mizo folk songs as symbols of beauty and resilience.
