# Article 371G — Constitutional Protection



**Article 371G** of the Constitution of India was inserted by the **State of Mizoram Act, 1986** as part of the [[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]] settlement.

It provides:
> No Act of Parliament in respect of **(a)** religious or social practices of the Mizos, **(b)** Mizo customary law and procedure, **(c)** administration of civil and criminal justice involving decisions according to Mizo customary law, and **(d)** ownership and transfer of land — shall apply to Mizoram unless the Mizoram Legislative Assembly decides to adopt such an Act.

This provision is the legal cornerstone protecting Mizo culture and land rights from dilution by national legislation. It underpins ongoing debates over land reforms, forest rights, and the [[Inner_Line_Permit]] system.
