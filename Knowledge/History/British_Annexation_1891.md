# British Annexation of Mizoram (1891)



Following the [[Lushai_Expeditions]], the British formally annexed Mizo territory in **1891**, administering it as two separate districts:

- **North Lushai Hills** — under the administration of Assam.
- **South Lushai Hills** — under Bengal.

In **1898**, the two were merged into the unified **Lushai Hills District** under Assam.

The British introduced:
- A formal revenue system replacing the Fathang tribute.
- A network of mule roads and later motor roads.
- The Inner Line Permit (ILP) system — restricting access by outsiders — which still remains in force today.
