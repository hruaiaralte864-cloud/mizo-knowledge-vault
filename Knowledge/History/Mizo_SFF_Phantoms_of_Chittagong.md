# The Special Frontier Force (SFF): Phantoms of Chittagong



The **Special Frontier Force (SFF)**, also known as **Establishment 22**, is a secretive paramilitary unit under the direct supervision of the Cabinet Secretariat and R&AW.

## The Mizo Connection
During the **[[Bangladesh_Liberation_War_1971]]**, Mizo soldiers were heavily recruited into the SFF for their expertise in jungle guerrilla warfare. They were instrumental in the "Phantoms of Chittagong" operations, where they conducted high-stakes sabotage behind Pakistani lines in the Chittagong Hill Tracts.

## High-Altitude Expertise
Because of their upbringing in the Mizo ridges, these soldiers provided the SFF with the organic "Mountain Intelligence" needed for operations against external threats in the Himalayas.

## Legacy of Secrecy
Due to the covert nature of the SFF, many Mizo veterans of this unit remained "unsung" for decades. Their history is only now being integrated into the broader **[[Mizo_Military_History_Archives]]**.
