# The Tiddim Road: A Military Lifeline



The **Tiddim Road** (extending from Imphal into the Chin Hills and through the fringes of Mizoram) was one of the most strategically significant roads in Southeast Asia during World War II.

## The Axis of Invasion
During **Operation U-Go** (1944), the Japanese 33rd Division used the Tiddim Road as their primary southern invasion axis toward Imphal. The road's narrow, winding nature through the mountain ridges made it the site of some of the most intense close-quarter combat in the theater.

## Archaeological Remains
Today, the legacy of the road is visible in the remains of Allied airfields and supply dumps scattered along the ridges. Villages near the border in the **[[Champhai_District]]** still contain "scrapped" military hardware from that era, which has been repurposed into traditional farm tools—a unique example of "Swords to Plowshares" in Mizo history.

## Modern Trade Route
The Tiddim Road’s historic path is the precursor to the modern trade routes found in the **[[Zokhawthar_and_Zorinpui_Land_Ports]]**. It serves as a reminder that the "Act East Policy" is built on the hard-won engineering and blood of the 1940s hills campaign.
