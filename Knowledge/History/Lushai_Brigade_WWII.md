# The Lushai Brigade: WWII in the Hills



The **Lushai Brigade** was an ad-hoc, mobile infantry formation of the British Indian Army raised in 1944. It played a critical, though often overlooked, role in protecting the southern flank of the Allied forces during the Battle of Imphal and the wider Burma Campaign.

## Formation and Leadership
Under the command of **Brigadier P.C. Marindin**, the brigade was formed to prevent Japanese infiltration through the rugged Lushai Hills into Silchar and the Assam plains. It consisted of several Indian infantry battalions and was supported by the local **[[Mizoram_Police_Commandos]]** precursors and the "V Force" of local Mizo scouts.

## Guerilla Warfare in a Vertical Terrain
Lacking heavy artillery, the brigade specialized in extreme "mountain guerilla warfare." They used the dense bamboo forests as cover to conduct "hit and run" raids on Japanese supply outposts. This strategy relied heavily on the knowledge of Mizo scouts, who knew the hidden "ridge-to-river" pathways that were unmapped by the British.

## Impact on the Burma Campaign
By disrupting the Japanese lines of communication in the south, the Lushai Brigade prevented a potential "second front" from opening up, allowing the main Allied forces to focus on the Siege of Kohima and Imphal. The bravery of Mizo soldiers during this period is exemplified by figures like **[[Major_Kapthuama_The_WWII_Hero]]**.
