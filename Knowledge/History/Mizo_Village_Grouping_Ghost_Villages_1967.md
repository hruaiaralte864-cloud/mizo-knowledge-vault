# Ghost Villages: The 1967 Village Grouping Legacy



The **Village Grouping** (1967-1970) was a controversial military operation during the insurgency where ~80% of native villages were uprooted and merged into "Protected Village Centers" (PVCs).

## Mapping the Ghost Sites
Thousands of traditional village sites on high ridges were abandoned. Today, these "Ghost Villages" are overgrown by jungle but remain in the communal memory of families. Archaeological surveys of these sites provide data on pre-grouping **[[Mizo_Clan_Hegemony_Sailo_Rise]]** patterns.

## Psychological Impact
The grouping caused a permanent shift in Mizo social psychology—moving from a decentralized "Clan Ridge" identity to a centralized "Urban Ridge" identity (Modern Aizawl and Lunglei).
