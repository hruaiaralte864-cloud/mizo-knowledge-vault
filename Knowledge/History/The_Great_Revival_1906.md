# The Great Mizo Revival (1906)

**Tags:** #history #religion #christianity

The **Great Revival of 1906** was a spectacular, mass religious awakening that transformed Mizo Christianity from a small missionary enterprise into a deep, indigenous, emotionally powerful movement.

## What Happened
Beginning among young men in the village of **Fatu** and rapidly spreading, the revival was characterised by:
- Spontaneous, hours-long weeping and public confession of sins.
- Glossolalia (speaking in tongues).
- Mass simultaneous conversions of whole villages.
- A feeling of overwhelming collective spiritual ecstasy.

The Welsh missionaries who witnessed it compared it to the **Welsh Revival of 1904–05** — the great religious awakening sweeping their own homeland at the same time — and sent reports back to Wales describing it as a miracle.

## Significance
The Revival shattered the residual authority of the **[[Pre-Christian_Animism|Bawlpu (shamans)]]** almost overnight. Within a decade, the majority of the Mizo population had converted. The Revival created the deeply emotional, communal, singing-focused character of [[Christianity_in_Mizoram|Mizo Christianity]] that persists today.
