﻿# Legal Node: Khurual Bride Price Law



## 1. Jurisprudential Classification
- **Category**: Marriage Law
- **Authority**: Customary Law
- **Core Rule**: Mandatory price paid to the bride's father/clan.

## 2. Judicial Context
The Khurual Bride Price Law is a foundational pillar of **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition]]**. Unlike Western adversarial law, traditional Mizo justice focuses on **Restorative Harmony**—ensuring that the social fabric of the village remains intact through mediation and symbolic compensation.

## 3. Legal Philosophy
Legalized the union and secured the status of children. In the ancient **[[Village_Council_Structure|Chief's Court]]**, the primary goal was the preservation of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**. A crime was not just an offense against an individual, but a disruption of the community's collective peace.

## 4. Modern Standing
Under the **[[Mizoram_State_Governance_Model]]** and the **[[Modern_Codification_1956|1956 Act]]**, these customary laws are officially recognized by the Indian Constitution (Article 371G).
