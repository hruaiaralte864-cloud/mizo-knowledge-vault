# Operation Jericho — Hour by Hour

**Tags:** #history #military #insurgency

The night of **28 February 1966** was the most dramatic in post-independence Mizo history. The [[Mizo_National_Front|MNF]]'s "Operation Jericho" was a stunning, simultaneous, multi-point military offensive.

## Timeline
- **10:00 PM, Feb 28:** MNF armed columns simultaneously attack Indian Army and Assam Rifles posts across 17 locations.
- **Midnight:** The treasury in [[Aizawl_District|Aizawl]] is captured. MNF cadres seize weapons and ammunition.
- **2:00 AM, Mar 1:** The Post and Telegraph office is seized, cutting all communication out of the territory.
- **Dawn, Mar 1:** MNF flags fly over Aizawl. [[Laldenga]] declares independence of "Mizoram."
- **Mar 5–6:** Indian Air Force aircraft (Vampire jets and Toofani fighters) bomb and strafe residential areas of Aizawl — the only time an Indian government bombed its own civilian population.
- **Mar 7:** Regular Indian Army battalions enter Aizawl, re-establishing control street by street.

## Aftermath
Though Operation Jericho failed militarily within days, it succeeded politically — it made it impossible for the Indian government to ignore the Mizo question, and began the 20-year saga that ultimately resulted in the [[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]].
