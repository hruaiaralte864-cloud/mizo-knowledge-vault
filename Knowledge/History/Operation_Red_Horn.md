# Operation Red Horn



**Operation Red Horn** was the codename for the Indian Army's large-scale military counter-insurgency operation launched in **March 1966** to retake the Lushai Hills from the [[Mizo_National_Front]].

## Context
Following the [[MNF_Uprising_1966|Operation Jericho]], where the MNF seized almost all major towns, the Indian government declared the region a "disturbed area."

## The Offensive
The operation involved multiple battalions of the Indian Army and the Assam Rifles. They fought through heavy jungle and contested ridges to re-establish control over Aizawl, Lunglei, and the border areas. It was during this operation that the controversial aerial bombing of Aizawl occurred.

## Aftermath
While Operation Red Horn succeeded in pushing the MNF cadres into the deep jungles and across the borders, it also marked the beginning of a 20-year low-intensity conflict that deeply scarred the Mizo psyche.
