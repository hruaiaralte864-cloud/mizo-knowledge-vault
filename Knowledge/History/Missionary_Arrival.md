# The Arrival of Christian Missionaries (1894)



The two most influential men in Mizo history never fired a gun. **J.H. Lorrain** (*Pu Buanga*) and **F.W. Savidge** (*Sap Upa*) of the Arthington Aborigines Mission arrived in [[Aizawl_District|Aizawl]] on **11 January 1894**.

Within months of arrival, they began constructing the Mizo alphabet — a romanised script adapted for the tonal Mizo language. In just four years they published:
- The first Mizo primer (*Thu leh Hla* — Words and Songs).
- The first Mizo–English dictionary.
- Early biblical translations.

Their work was later complemented by the **Welsh Presbyterian Mission** (1897) and the **American Baptist Mission** in the south.
