# The Lushai Expeditions (1871–1890)



British military expeditions into Mizo territory were motivated by cross-border raids on tea plantations.

## First Expedition (1871–72)
Triggered by the Alexandrapur Tea Estate raid where [[Khuangchera_Warrior|Bengkhuaia's]] clan killed planter James Winchester and kidnapped his daughter Mary (*Zoluti*). A joint column from Chittagong and Cachar penetrated the hills, rescued Mary, and burned multiple villages.

## Chin-Lushai Expedition (1889–90)
A far larger, more decisive campaign. British columns advanced deep into the hills. The Mizo warrior **[[Khuangchera]]** and his companion Ngurbawnga were killed in the battle of Changsil while defending against the British advance. This expedition resulted in the full annexation of Mizo territory.
