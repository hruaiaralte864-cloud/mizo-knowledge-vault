# Pre-Colonial Mizo Society



Before British annexation in 1891, Mizo society was organized entirely around the autonomous [[Village_System|village]] under a hereditary chief called the **[[Lal_System|Lal]]**.

There was no writing system, no currency, and no formal state. Social cohesion was maintained through the [[Zawlbuk]] institution, enforced by the strict code of [[Tlawmngaihna]], and celebrated through the [[Kut_Festivals]].

Farming was exclusively [[Jhum_Cultivation|Jhum]] (slash-and-burn). Protein came from hunting wild animals, and the prestige ranking system of **[[Thangchhuah]]** motivated excellence in both hunting and feasting.
