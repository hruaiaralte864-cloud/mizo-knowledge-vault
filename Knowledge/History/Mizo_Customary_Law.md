# Mizo Customary Law



Mizo Customary Law (*Mizo Dân*) is the body of uncodified, orally transmitted legal norms governing social relations among the Mizo people. Protected by [[Article_371G]], it governs:

- **Marriage & Divorce:** The [[Man_Bride_Price]] system is deeply embedded in customary law, with precise formulas for payments and returns on divorce.
- **Inheritance:** Traditionally patrilineal. The youngest son (*Fapa thim*) inherits the parents' house and is responsible for their care in old age.
- **Land:** Under the Lal system, all land belonged to the Chief. Post-colonial land tenure is a complex residual of customary rights and modern legislation.
- **Village Disputes:** Mediated by the Village Council (*Khua Pawl*) according to oral precedent.
