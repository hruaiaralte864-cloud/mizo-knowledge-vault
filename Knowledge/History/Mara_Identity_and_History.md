# The Mara Identity and History

**Tags:** #history #culture #mara #ethnic_diversity

The **Mara people** (traditionally referred to as Shendus by early British chroniclers) are one of the most distinct ethnic groups within the **[[Zohnahthlak]]** family. They primarily inhabit the southern-most part of Mizoram in the **[[Siaha_District]]**.

## Unique Origins and Language
The Mara trace their origin to the "Kabaw Valley" and have a linguistic tradition that is distinct from the Lusei-Mizo majority. The Mara language has several sub-dialects like *Tlosai*, *Hlaipao*, and *Zeuhno*. Unlike the northern tribes who were influenced by Welsh missionaries, the Mara were primarily reached by the Lakher Pioneer Mission, leading to a distinct ecclesiastical and literary culture.

## Social Structure and the Mara Council
The Mara social structure was traditionally dominated by powerful chiefs. Today, this is formalized through the **[[Siaha_Mara_Council_House]]** and the **[[Mara_Autonomous_District_Council_MADC]]**. The Mara are known for their preservation of traditional laws regarding land and marriage, which are often distinct from the general **[[Mizo_Customary_Law_Registry]]**.

## Cultural Markers
The Mara are famous for their unique dances like the "Solokia" (a majestic warrior dance) and their intricate beadwork and weaponry. Their traditional festivals, such as *Pakhupila*, are major events that highlight the diversity within **[[Mizo_Cultural_Identity]]**.
