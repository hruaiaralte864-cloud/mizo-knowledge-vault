# The Zopui Village Skirmish (1888)

**Tags:** #history #war #colonial

The **Zopui Skirmish** was a fierce military encounter between the Mizo tribal warriors and the British South Lushai Expedition forces, marking the beginning of the end for sovereign **[[Sailo_Chiefs]]** in the south.

## Background
The British were attempting to establish a "telegraph road" connecting Chittagong to the **[[Lunglei_District]]**. The Zopui clan, led by powerful local headmen, resisted the intrusion into their hunting grounds and the forced labor demands of the **[[Lushai_Expeditions_Logistics]]**.

## The Battle
Using the dense jungle canopy as cover, the Zopui warriors attacked a British surveying party. They used traditional bone-tipped arrows and a few smuggled percussion guns. The British retaliated with superior firepower, eventually burning the Zopui village to the ground.

## Historical Significance
The Zopui resistance is cited in **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]** as a symbol of southern Mizo pride. The defeat led to the establishment of the British fort at Lunglei and the subsequent transition of the south into the British Raj's "administered territories." It is a key chapter in the **[[Lunglei_District]]**'s colonial history.
