# Village Grouping — Khawkhawm

**Tags:** #history #insurgency #human_rights

The **Village Grouping** programme (Mizo: *Khawkhawm* — "huddled villages") was the Indian military's primary counter-insurgency strategy during the [[MNF_Uprising_1966]].

Between 1966 and 1971, hundreds of remote villages were forcibly evacuated. Army units burned the thatched longhouses, granaries, and sometimes churches. Entire communities were relocated into government-designated **Protected and Progressive Villages (PPVs)** built along the main highways where they could be monitored.

## Human Cost
- Over 80% of Mizoram's population was uprooted.
- Agricultural production collapsed. Famine conditions returned.
- Communities lost ancestral burial sites, orchards, and centuries of cultural geography.
- [[Brigadier_T_Sailo]] famously documented these abuses to the Prime Minister.
