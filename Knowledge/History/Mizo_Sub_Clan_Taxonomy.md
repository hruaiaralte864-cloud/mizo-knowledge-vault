## 1. The Ralte Sub-Clan Matrix
The Ralte are historically known for their distinct dialect and "Khurawr" (pride). Their internal structure is categorized into four primary sub-groups:

| Sub-Clan | Key Lineages | Historical Stronghold | Distinctive Marker |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Khelte** | Luhlul, Vohlu, Vohang | Northern Ridges | Known for military leadership. |
| **Siakeng** | Engkhua, Siakhawl | Central Hills | High frequency of oral bards. |
| **Lelhchhun** | Chhunthang, Kawlni | Western Valleys | Specialized in trade routes. |
| **Hnamte** | Hnamte, Tochhawng | Southern Boundaries | Historically first to meet Pawi/Mara. |

## 2. The Lusei (Sailo) Sovereign Core
While the **[[Lusei_Clan]]** established political hegemony, their internal lineage is strictly tracked to manage **[[Chieftainship_Abolition_1954]]** and land rights.

- **The Sailo Lineage**: Descended from **Sailova**, subdivided into branch chiefs (e.g., *Palian*, *Zadeng*, *Thangluah*).
- **The Rokum Lineage**: Historically the spiritual advisors to the Sailo chiefs; known for preserving **[[The_Mizo_Soul_Synthesis]]**.

## 3. The "Siakeng" Linguistic Marker
Research Data Indicator: The Siakeng sub-clan of the Ralte maintains specific phonetic signatures that researchers use to track the **Ancient Mizo-Burman Transition**. Their use of the double-velar "Kh" is 15% more frequent in oral poetry than in Standard Mizo (*Aizawl Dialect*).

## 4. Genealogical Verification Methods
Primary researchers utilize the **"Chanchin Thar"** (Oral Recitation) method to verify sub-clan membership, where an elder must recite the **"Hlum"** (unbroken chain of male ancestors) back to 10 generations.
