# Rajiv Gandhi and the Mizo Peace Process

**Tags:** #history #politics #peace

The final breakthrough in the long [[MNF_Uprising_1966|Mizo insurgency]] came under **Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi**, who took personal interest in resolving the Northeast's conflicts.

## Back-Channel Talks
By the early 1980s, both sides were exhausted. [[Laldenga]], operating from exile with gradually diminishing international support, opened secret back-channel communications with the Indian government through intermediaries including the Congress party's Mizoram leadership under **[[Lalthanhawla]]**.

## The Final Negotiations (1985–1986)
A series of meetings in New Delhi between Laldenga and Home Ministry officials progressively resolved the major sticking points:
1. The demand for a separate nation was abandoned in exchange for full Statehood.
2. General amnesty was agreed for all MNF surrendering combatants.
3. Constitutional protections for Mizo cultural law were codified as **[[Article_371G]]**.

## Signing (June 30, 1986)
The [[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]] was signed at the PM's residence in New Delhi. Rajiv Gandhi reportedly described it as "one of the most successful peace processes in Indian history."
