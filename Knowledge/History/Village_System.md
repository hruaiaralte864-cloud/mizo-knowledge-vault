# The Traditional Mizo Village

**Tags:** #history #society #architecture

A traditional Mizo village (*Khua*) was always built on a high ridge for defensive advantage. Key structures:

- **Chief's House (*Lal In*):** Central, largest structure, symbol of power.
- **[[Zawlbuk]]:** The bachelors' dormitory, adjacent to the Chief's house. The nerve center of village defense and education.
- **Granary (*Hlum*):** Communal storage built on stilts.
- **Well (*Tui*):** Usually a spring downhill, heavily protected.

Villages were typically small — 20 to 100 households — due to limited agricultural land and the need for rapid military mobility.
