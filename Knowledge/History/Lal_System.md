# The Lal (Chieftain) System

**Tags:** #history #governance #culture

The **Lal** (Chief) was the absolute ruler of a Mizo village. All farmland belonged to him, and he distributed Jhum plots annually to villagers. His authority covered life, death, and land.

## Key Institutions
- **Fathang:** Annual tribute, typically a portion of the harvest, paid to the Lal.
- **Sachhiah:** A foreleg of any wild animal hunted by a villager was presented to the Chief.
- **Bawi System:** Individuals unable to clear debts could become the Chief's dependents (*Bawi*) in exchange for his protection and food.
- **Upa:** Council of village elders who advised the Lal on disputes and administration.

The Chief's compound, beside the [[Zawlbuk]], formed the heart of every village.
