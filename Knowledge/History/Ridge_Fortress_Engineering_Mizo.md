# Ridge Fortress Engineering: Ancient Warfare



Mizo villages were historically "Vertical Fortresses." The choice of ridge-top village sites was driven by a sophisticated military logic of "Impregnability."

## Stockade Design
Villages were surrounded by a double stockade (*Sahlam*) made of hardwood stakes and sharpened bamboo (*Khawh-thlir*). The entrance was a narrow, steep path that could be defended by a single warrior using the **[[Mizo_Weapons_Science_Chempui_Crossbow]]**.

## The Ridge Defense Philosophy
By building on the highest point, Mizo chiefs (Lal) ensured 360-degree visibility over the valleys. Ambushes were planned in the "V-shaped" valleys, trapping enemies on the narrow slopes. This tactical advantage allowed warriors like **[[Khuangchera]]** to hold off significantly larger British forces.

## The Zawlbuk as Command Center
The **[[Zawlbuk_Mizo_Bachelors_Dormitory]]** was always located at the highest point of the village, serving as a ready-response barracks for the village defenders.
