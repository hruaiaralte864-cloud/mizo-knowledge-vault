# The Alexandrapur Tea Estate Raid (1871)

**Tags:** #history #british #conflict

The most consequential single event triggering formal British military intervention in Mizo territory was the raid on the **Alexandrapur Tea Estate** in January 1871.

The Mizo chief **Bengkhuaia** led his warriors across the Cachar plains and attacked the plantation. The Scottish planter **James Winchester** was killed, and his young daughter **Mary Winchester** (later named *Zoluti* — "the foreign flower" — by the Mizo) was taken as a captive.

## Mary Winchester's Captivity
Mary lived among Bengkhuaia's clan for months and was reportedly well-treated, learning fragments of the Mizo language. The British colonial government, under enormous public pressure from the press in Calcutta and London, was forced to mount a military expedition to rescue her.

This triggered the **[[Lushai_Expeditions|First Lushai Expedition of 1871–72]]**, fundamentally changing the trajectory of Mizo history by drawing the British Empire directly into the hills.
