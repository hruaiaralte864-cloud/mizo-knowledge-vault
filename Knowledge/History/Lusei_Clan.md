# Lusei Clan



The **Lusei** (or *Lushai*) is the dominant clan of the Mizo people. They were historically the most powerful and influential group, and their dialect (*Lusei ṭawng*) became the basis for the modern standardised Mizo language.

## Historical Dominance
From the 18th century onwards, the Lusei chiefs (specifically the **Sailo** lineage) established a near-monopoly on governance across the Lushai Hills. They were known for their administrative skills and military prowess. By the time the British arrived, Lusei chiefs ruled over the vast majority of the hill villages.

## Characteristics
The Lusei were instrumental in consolidating the various tribes into a unified identity. Their social structures, such as the [[Village_System]] and the [[Lal_System]], became the blueprint for other clans. Historically, to be "Lusei" was synonymous with being part of the ruling elite.

## Connection to Language
Because of their political power, the Welsh missionaries chose the Lusei dialect to translate the Bible and create the [[Mizo_Alphabet]]. This cemented Lusei dominance in the cultural and literary sphere of modern Mizoram.
