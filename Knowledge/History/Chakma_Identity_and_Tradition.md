# The Chakma Identity and Tradition

**Tags:** #history #culture #chakma #buddhism

The **Chakma people** of Mizoram represent a unique cultural and religious enclave within the state. Primarily practitioners of Theravada Buddhism, they mainly inhabit the southwestern corner of Mizoram in the **[[Mamit_District]]** and parts of Lawngtlai.

## Linguistic and Religious Roots
Unlike the Tibeto-Burman Mizo groups, the Chakma language is an Indo-Aryan tongue related to Bengali and Assamese. Their religious life centers around the *Vihara* (Buddhist temple), providing a distinct spiritual contrast to the majority Christian **[[History_Overview]]**.

## Governance: The CADC
The Chakma are self-governed through the **[[Chakma_Autonomous_District_Council_CADC]]**. This body manages local schools, markets, and land distribution. The Chakma have a long history of shifting cultivation and are experts in water-resource management along the rivers bordering Bangladesh.

## Cultural Festivals: Bijhu
The most important cultural event for the Chakma is the **Bijhu** festival, marking the New Year. It is a three-day celebration involving floral offerings, traditional sports, and the distinctive "Bijhu Dance." This festival is an integral part of the southern **[[Geography_Overview]]** cultural calendar.
