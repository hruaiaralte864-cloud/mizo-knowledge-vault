# The Ratu Massacre: History and Folklore



The **Ratu Massacre** is a dark and pivotal event in pre-colonial Mizo history, often cited in oral folk songs as a warning against inter-clan betrayal.

## Incident and Context
The massacre occurred during a period of intense rivalry between the Sailo chiefs and other Mizo clans. According to lore, a large number of villagers were lured into a trap under the guise of a peace feast (*Kut*). The ensuing violence decimated the village population and led to a multi-generational feud that reshaped the power dynamics of the northern hills.

## Cultural Reflection
In the **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]**, the Ratu incident is used to explain the abandonment of certain village sites and the migration of clans toward the south. It is often referenced in **[[Hlado_And_Music]]** as a symbol of loss and the fragility of peace in the "time of the chiefs" (*Lal lai*).

## Historical Record
While primarily preserved through oral history, archaeological findings of mass burial sites near Ratu village provide a physical anchor to the legend. It serves as a stark reminder of the social turbulence that preceded the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**'s modern stability.
