# The Hmar Identity: Diaspora and Cultural Union

**Tags:** #history #culture #hmar #lineage

The **Hmar people** are one of the most geographically spread-out groups of the **[[Zohnahthlak]]** family, with significant populations in Mizoram, Manipur, Assam, and Meghalaya.

## Historical Migration
The Hmar share the "Sinlung" origin myth with the Lusei but migrated through distinct northern routes. Within Mizoram, they are concentrated in the **[[Aizawl_District]]** and the northern hills near the Manipur border. Their history is defined by a strong sense of intellectual achievement—the Hmar were among the **[[First_Mizo_Graduates]]**.

## Literary Heritage
The Hmar have a rich literary tradition. The Hmar literature movement in the mid-20th century was a precursor to the modern **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters_MAL]]**. They have their own distinct version of the *Kut* festivals, emphasizing clan unity and choral singing.

## The Hmar-Lusei Relation
While the Hmar maintain a distinct sub-identity, they have been primary drivers of the "Greater Mizo" movement. Many Hmar leaders were instrumental in the **[[Mizo_National_Front]]** uprising and the subsequent **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**, advocating for the total reunification of all Zo tribes.
