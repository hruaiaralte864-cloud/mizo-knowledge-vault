# Mizo Labour Corps (WWI)



During World_War_I, in **1917**, approximately **2,100 Mizo men** were recruited into the **27th Lushai Labour Corps** to serve the British Empire in France.

## The Journey
These men, who had never seen a city or the ocean, were transported by train to Calcutta and then by ship to the Western Front. Their primary role was to dig trenches, build roads, and handle logistics behind the lines of the brutal conflict in France.

## Impact on Return
The survivors returned to the Lushai Hills in 1918. They brought back more than just stories; they brought a new perspective on the world.
- **Global Awareness:** They realized that the British were not invincible.
- **Literacy:** Many learned to read and write more fluently during their service.
- **Unity:** Men from different clans worked together as one unit, which planted the early seeds of pan-Mizo nationalism.

A memorial to the Mizo Labour Corps stands in Aizawl today, commemorating their service and sacrifice far from home.
