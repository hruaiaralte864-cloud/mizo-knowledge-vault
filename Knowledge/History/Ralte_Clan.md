# Ralte Clan



The **Ralte** are one of the most prominent Mizo clans, known historically for their distinct dialect, fierce independence, and a reputation for being vocal and energetic builders of community.

## Historical Position
The Ralte often lived in the northern and western parts of the hills. Unlike some smaller clans that were absorbed into the Lusei identity early on, the Ralte maintained a very strong sense of distinctiveness. They were often the primary rivals to Lusei expansion.

## Clan Psychology
In Mizo folklore, the Ralte are often jokingly described as being "too loud" or "always arguing" during communal work — a stereotype reflecting their strong individuality and refusal to be easily dominated by central authority. This characteristic is often celebrated today as a sign of democratic spirit.

## Sub-groups
The Ralte clan is divided into several sub-clans including the **Khelte, Siakeng, Lelhchhun**, and **Hnamte**. Each had their own traditional territories.
