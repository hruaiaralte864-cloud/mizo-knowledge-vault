# Mizo National Famine Front (MNFF) — 1959



The **Mizo National Famine Front (MNFF)** was the direct precursor to the [[Mizo_National_Front]]. It was founded by [[Laldenga]] in **1959** during the catastrophic [[Mautam_Famine_1959|Mautam bamboo famine]].

## Purpose
Originally a purely humanitarian organisation, the MNFF coordinated:
- Food distribution to starving villages.
- Medical relief for famine-affected areas.
- Petitions to the Assam government and central government for emergency food aid.

## Transformation
When the Assam government and Government of India largely ignored the Mizo people's suffering, the deep resentment that accumulated was channelled by Laldenga from famine relief into political mobilisation. In **1961**, he dropped "Famine" from the name, creating the **Mizo National Front (MNF)** with independence as its stated goal.
