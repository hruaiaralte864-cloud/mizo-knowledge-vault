# Logistics of the Lushai Expeditions (1871-1890)

**Tags:** #history #warfare #logistics

The **Lushai Expeditions** were a series of military punitive raids by the British Raj against various Mizo clans. While often viewed as military conflicts, their outcome was largely determined by the brutal logistical challenges of the Mizo terrain.

## The Difficult Terrain
British planning focused on the "Cachar-Lushai" route and the "Chittagong-Lushai" route. The logistics involved thousands of coolies (manual porters) carrying food, ammunition, and telegraph wires up near-vertical ridges. The lack of roads meant that military progress was often limited to 3-5 miles per day.

## Defensive Strategies
Mizo chiefs like **[[Chief_Lalchungnunga_Resistance]]** exploited these logistical bottlenecks. They practiced a "scorched earth" policy, burning grain stores and poisoning water sources in the path of the British advance. This forced the British to maintain enormous supply lines, which were constantly harassed by Mizo sharpshooters using percussion-lock guns.

## The Telegraph and Roads
Ultimately, the British "conquered" the hills not just with guns, but with infrastructure. They established a network of fortified posts and connected them with telegraph wires—the first instance of modern **[[Governance_Overview]]** infrastructure in the region. This paved the way for the eventual administrative mapping of the **[[Aizawl_District]]** and **[[Lunglei_District]]**.
