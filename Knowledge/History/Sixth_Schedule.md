# The Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution



The **Sixth Schedule** of the Indian Constitution (Article 244) provides special autonomous governance arrangements for tribal populations in four Northeast Indian states: Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura, and **Mizoram**.

## Autonomous District Councils in Mizoram
Under the Sixth Schedule, three **Autonomous District Councils (ADCs)** operate within Mizoram with extensive legislative, judicial, and executive powers:

| Council | Region | Primary Group |
|---|---|---|
| **Chakma ADC (CADC)** | Southern Lawngtlai | Chakma Buddhist community |
| **Mara ADC (MADC)** | Southern Siaha | Mara people |
| **Lai ADC (LADC)** | Parts of Lawngtlai | Lai (Pawi) people |

The ADCs can legislate on land use, forests, village administration, money-lending, social customs, and maintenance of schools.
