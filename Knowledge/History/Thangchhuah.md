# Thangchhuah — The Prestige System

**Tags:** #history #culture #prestige

**Thangchhuah** was the ultimate social honour a Mizo man could achieve in pre-colonial society. It could be earned through two paths:

**1. Inlam Thangchhuah (Feast Path):** Hosting escalating levels of public feasts for the entire village. The final and highest feast, *Khuangchawi*, required enormous wealth and resulted in the recipient wearing a distinctive **Thangchhuah kawn** (decorated warrior robe) for life.

**2. Rallam Thangchhuah (Hunt Path):** By personally killing six specific species of dangerous animals: elephant, tiger, wild boar, bear, gaur (Indian bison), and sambar deer.

A Thangchhuah man was believed to skip the purgatorial [[Mitthi_Khua]] and ascend directly to [[Pialral]] (paradise) upon death.
