# Constitutional Protection: Article 371G



**Article 371G** of the Constitution of India is the cornerstone of the state's legal relationship with the Union. Inserted via the 53rd Constitutional Amendment in 1986, it provides unique safeguards that are fundamental to the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**.

## The Protected Domains
Under Article 371G, no Act of the Indian Parliament applies to Mizoram in respect of the following four areas, unless the **[[Mizoram_Legislative_Assembly]]** decides so by a resolution:
1. **Religious or Social Practices**: Protects the unique Mizo Christian identity and community rituals.
2. **Mizo Customary Law and Procedure**: Ensures that traditional ways of resolving disputes remain valid (see **[[Mizo_Customary_Law_Registry]]**).
3. **Administration of Civil and Criminal Justice**: Protects the informal village court systems.
4. **Ownership and Transfer of Land**: Prevents non-indigenous people from owning land in the state, preserving the resource for future generations.

## Practical Implication: Land Reform
This article is what allows the **[[Land_Revenue_Settlement_Mizo]]** to operate as a closed system. It ensures that the state’s developmental goals, such as the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]**, are implemented while maintaining the territorial integrity of the Mizo people.

## Sovereignty of the House
Unlike other states where Central laws are supreme, in Mizoram, the State Assembly acts as a "buffer." This "Legislative Sovereignty" is a primary reason for the stable **[[Mizoram_Political_History]]** and the state’s high level of civic trust.
