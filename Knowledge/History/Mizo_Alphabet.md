# The Mizo Alphabet & Language



The Mizo language (**Mizo ṭawng**) belongs to the Tibeto-Burman language family, closely related to Kuki-Chin languages. Before 1894 it had no written form.

[[Missionary_Arrival|J.H. Lorrain and F.W. Savidge]] devised a romanised alphabet using 23 Latin characters to transcribe the language's sounds. As a largely non-tonal (stress-based) language, it adapted well to the Roman script.

The script created a revolution:
- **Literacy** spread rapidly via church-run schools.
- A rich **Mizo literature** tradition emerged within decades, producing legendary poets like [[Rokunga]] and novelists like [[L_Biakliana]].
- Newspapers, magazines, and eventually digital content blossomed.

Today Mizo is constitutionally recognised and is the medium of instruction in Mizoram's schools.
