# Political Node: Operation Jericho

**Tags:** #politics #history #independence #Mizoram #research #Diplomacy #MNF

## 1. Historical Classification
- **Category**: Military Event
- **Primary Location**: Aizawl
- **Key Timeline**: 1966

## 2. Event/Profile Context
The Operation Jericho is a pivotal element in the **[[Mizoram_State_Governance_Model|Political History of Mizoram]]**. It represents the struggle and eventual transition of the Zo people toward a recognized **[[Statehood_Day_Mizoram|Statehood]]** within the Indian Union.

## 3. Strategic Significance
The lightning strike by MNF volunteers to capture administrative centers in Aizawl. This event/entity significantly influenced the **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition]]**, shifting the focus from tribal chieftainships to a modern democratic identity led by figures like **[[Pu_Laldenga_Profile|Pu Laldenga]]**.

## 4. Legacy and Reconciliation
Post-**[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986|1986]]**, this history is preserved as a testament to the resilience of the Mizo people and their commitment to **[[Peace_Negotiators_Index|Peaceful Reconciliation]]**.
