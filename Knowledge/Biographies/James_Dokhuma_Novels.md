# The Novels of James Dokhuma: A Literary Analysis

**Tags:** #literature #arts #analysis

The novels of **James Dokhuma** are the bedrock of modern Mizo prose. They are praised for their psychological depth and their ability to weave traditional Mizo folklore into contemporary narratives.

## Major Works
- ***Thaduna leh Thangi***: An epic romance that explores the conflict between personal desire and tribal duty. It is considered the "Romeo and Juliet" of Mizo literature.
- ***Runlum Nupui***: A biting social critique of post-independence Mizo society, dealing with themes of corruption and the loss of traditional values (*[[Tlawmngaihna]]*).
- ***Zoram Khawvel***: (Though often categorized as a historical essay, its narrative depth is novelistic).

## Narrative Style
Dokhuma’s style was unique in its use of archaic Mizo terms alongside modern syntax. He sought to preserve the "high language" of the ancient bards while making it accessible to a new generation. His influence on the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters_MAL]]** ensured that the Mizo novel remained a serious medium for social and spiritual exploration.

## Legacy
His novels are part of the curriculum in schools across the state, ensuring that his vision of the **[[Mizo_Cultural_Identity]]** remains active in the minds of the youth.
