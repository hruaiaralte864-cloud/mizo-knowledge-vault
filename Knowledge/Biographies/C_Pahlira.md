# C. Pahlira (Mizo Social Reformer)



**C. Pahlira** was a militant social reformer who played an essential role in the movement against the oppressive aspects of the hereditary chieftainship in the mid-20th century.

## The Anti-Chief Movement
Pahlira was a core leader of the Mizo Union, the first political party in the Lushai Hills. He campaigned vigorously for the abolition of the **[[Lal_System]]**, which he argued was an obstacle to democracy and social equality. His efforts were instrumental in the passing of the Lushai Hills District (Acquisition of Chiefs' Rights) Act in 1954.

## Human Rights and Equality
Beyond political reform, Pahlira was a deep proponent of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]** in its most egalitarian form. He worked to ensure that the "common man" (*mizantlang*) had a voice in the new democratic structures of the district council. His work in **[[Pahlira_The_Abolitionist]]** and his advocacy for human rights are still discussed in Mizo political science today.

## Legacy
He is remembered as a "brave heart" who stood up to traditional power structures to pave the way for modern **[[Governance_Overview]]** in Mizoram.
