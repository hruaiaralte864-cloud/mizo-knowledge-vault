# Kaphleia — The Poet of Sorrow (1887–1954)

**Tags:** #biography #literature #poetry

**Kaphleia** (Rev. Liangkhaia) occupies a unique and deeply emotional place in Mizo literary history. Where [[Rokunga]] celebrates the beauty of the land and [[Vankhama]] composed songs of joy, Kaphleia is the poet of longing, grief, and spiritual yearning.

## Literary Voice
He composed deeply melancholic lyric poems and hymns that captured the spiritual disorientation of a people caught between two worlds — the vibrant but fear-ridden animist past and the hope-filled but dismantling Christian present. His compositions are filled with imagery of mist, rivers, abandoned villages, and loss.

## Significance
Kaphleia's hymns are among the most performed in Mizo churches today. His most famous works speak of heaven (*[[Pialral]]*) with an urgent, aching longing that resonates with Mizo congregations' sense of being sojourners in an impermanent land.
