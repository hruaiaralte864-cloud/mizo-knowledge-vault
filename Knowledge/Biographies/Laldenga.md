# Laldenga (1927–1990)

**Tags:** #biography #politics #mizo

**Laldenga** (full name: Laldenga) was born in 1927 in Pukpui village, Lunglei. He is the defining political figure of modern Mizo history — first as the architect of the MNF insurgency, then as the statesman who chose peace.

## Early Life
He served as a soldier in the Assam Regiment and later worked as a clerk. He was a powerfully charismatic speaker with an exceptional ability to organise people around a cause.

## The Insurgency Years
Laldenga founded the **[[Mizo_National_Front]]** (1961) in the aftermath of the [[Mautam_Famine_1959]]. Under his leadership, the MNF launched **[[MNF_Uprising_1966|Operation Jericho]]** in 1966. He spent the next two decades in exile — in East Pakistan, Myanmar, and Pakistan.

## The Peacemaker
After years of secret back-channel negotiations, Laldenga signed the [[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]] with Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi in June 1986. He returned home to massive public celebration and became **Mizoram's Chief Minister** in 1987 following electoral victory.

He died in office in **1990**, widely mourned as the Father of the Mizo Nation.
