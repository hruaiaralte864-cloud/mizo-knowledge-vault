# James Dokhuma: The Literary Giant



**James Dokhuma** (1932–2007) was a towering figure in Mizo literature and social history. A Padma Shri awardee, his works are considered essential reading for anyone seeking to understand the Mizo soul and the turbulence of the 20th century.

## Literary Range
Dokhuma was a versatile writer, excelling in novels, poetry, and historical essays. His most famous novel, *Thaduna leh Thangi*, is a classic of Mizo romanticism, while his historical works on the **[[Mizo_Insurgency_Movement]]** provide a rare insider's perspective on the conflict. He was a cornerstone of the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters_MAL]]**.

## The Resistance and Prison
During the years of insurgency, Dokhuma was a member of the subterranean movement and spent time in prison. It was during these periods of confinement that he produced some of his most profound poetry, reflecting on themes of freedom, faith, and the **[[Mizo_Cultural_Identity]]**.

## Honors and Impact
He was awarded the Padma Shri in 1985 for his contribution to literature and education. His influence on modern Mizo writers is immense, as he modernized the Mizo narrative style while keeping it rooted in **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]**.
