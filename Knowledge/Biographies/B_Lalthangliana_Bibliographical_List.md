# B. Lalthangliana: The Bibliographical Custodian



One of the most significant yet understated contributions of **B. Lalthangliana** is his meticulous documentation of Mizo literature and historical records.

## Organizing the Mizo Corpus
Before Lalthangliana, many Mizo historical records were scattered and uncatalogued. He spent decades visiting remote villages and archives in London and Kolkata to create a comprehensive bibliographical list of all known Mizo writings. This work became the foundation for the **[[Library_of_Mizo_Literature]]**.

## Preserving Orality
He was one of the first scholars to argue that folk songs (**[[Hlado_And_Music]]**) should be treated as rigorous primary sources for history. His bibliographical lists include rare transcriptions of ancient chants that were on the verge of being forgotten.

## Academic Utility
Today, his bibliographies are essential for any PhD student or researcher in the field of Mizo studies. He provided the metadata for the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters_MAL]]** records, ensuring that the intellectual history of the tribe is searchable and preserved for posterity.
