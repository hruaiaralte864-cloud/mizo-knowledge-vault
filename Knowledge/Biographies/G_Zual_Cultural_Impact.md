# G. Zuala: The Cultural Bridge

**Tags:** #biography #culture #media

**G. Zuala** was a legendary figure in Mizo media and cultural preservation, known for his work in documenting and promoting Mizo heritage through radio and film.

## Broadcasting Excellence
During his long career at All India Radio (AIR) Aizawl, G. Zuala produced groundbreaking programs that explored **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]** and folk history. His voice became synonymous with the "Quiet Hours" of Mizo radio, where he would narrate tales of ancient chiefs and heroes.

## Film and Visual Media
He was a pioneer in the early Mizo film industry, helping to produce some of the first documentaries that captured traditional dances and rituals in high detail. His work ensured that many aspects of **[[Chheih_Lam]]** and **[[Cheraw_Dance]]** were preserved for future generations.

## Legacy
G. Zuala's impact is seen in the continued popularity of cultural programs on Mizo television today. He was a true custodian of the **[[Mizo_Cultural_Identity]]**.
