# Brigadier T. Sailo: International Human Rights Advocacy

**Tags:** #biography #governance #human_rights

**Brigadier T. Sailo** was a pioneer in the defense of human rights in Mizoram during the height of the **[[Mizo_Insurgency_Movement]]**. His military background allowed him to speak with authority and courage against the excesses committed during the counter-insurgency operations.

## The Human Rights Committee
In 1974, he founded the Human Rights Committee, the first organization of its kind in the region. He meticulously documented cases of "grouping" (villagization) and other civil liberty violations. He presented these findings directly to the higher echelons of the Indian government and international observers, forcing a shift in policy toward a more "hearts and minds" approach.

## Legacy of "Clean Politics"
His advocacy was not limited to human rights; it evolved into a crusade for **[[Brigadier_Sailo_Clean_Politics]]**. He argued that true peace could only be achieved if governance was transparent and accountable. This philosophy heavily influenced the **[[Governance_Overview]]** of the subsequent decades and remains a benchmark for Mizo political ethics.

## Impact on the Peace Process
By holding both the underground and the security forces accountable, Sailo created the moral space necessary for the signing of the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**. He is remembered as a soldier-statesman who prioritized the dignity of the common citizen over political or military gain.
