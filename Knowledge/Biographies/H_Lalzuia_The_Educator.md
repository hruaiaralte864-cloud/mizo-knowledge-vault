# H. Lalzuia (The Educator of Lunglei)



**H. Lalzuia** was a pioneering educator who dedicated his life to spreading literacy in southern Mizoram, particularly in the **[[Lunglei_District]]**.

## Founding Principles
At a time when education was sparse outside of Aizawl, Lalzuia established some of the first non-missionary schools in the region. He believed that education should be accessible to all, regardless of their ability to pay. He was a master of the **[[Mizo_Alphabet]]** and spent decades teaching both youth and adults.

## Community Service
His work was deeply rooted in the concept of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**. He often volunteered his time to tutor students from remote villages who traveled to Lunglei for schooling. His legacy is reflected in the high literacy rankings of **[[Lunglei_District]]** today.

## Recognition
He is remembered not just as a teacher, but as a moral guide who instilled a sense of duty and history in generations of Mizo leaders.
