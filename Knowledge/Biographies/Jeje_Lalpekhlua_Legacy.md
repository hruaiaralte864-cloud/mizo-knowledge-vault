# Jeje Lalpekhlua: The "Mizo Sniper"



**Jeje Lalpekhlua**, hailing from Hnahthial, is widely considered the greatest Mizo footballer to have graced the national stage. His legendary career earned him the nickname "Mizo Sniper" for his precision and composure in front of the goal.

## National Stature
Jeje was a mainstay of the Indian National Team for over a decade, becoming one of its highest-rated goalscorers. He played for top-tier clubs like Mohun Bagan and Chennaiyin FC, winning multiple Indian Super League (ISL) and I-League titles. His success was a primary driver for the **[[Aizawl_FC_I_League_Win]]** inspiration.

## Impact on Mizo Youth
Beyond his stats, Jeje’s legacy lies in the belief he instilled in Mizo athletes. He proved that a player from a small hill town could compete globally. This paved the way for the "Mizo Wave" in Indian football, with nearly every ISL team now featuring at least one Mizo player.

## Post-Retirement
Since retiring, Jeje has been active in local governance and sports administration, advocating for better training facilities like the **[[Synthetic_Track_Development_Sports]]** initiatives. He remains a national icon and a symbol of Mizo physical excellence.
