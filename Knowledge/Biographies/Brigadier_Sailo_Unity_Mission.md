# Brigadier T. Sailo: The Peoples' Unity Mission

**Tags:** #biography #governance #society

The **Unity Mission** was a grassroots social and political movement launched by **[[Brigadier_Sailo_International_Human_Rights]]** during the peak of the insurgency era. Its goal was to unify the fragmented Mizo society and provide a clear, non-violent path toward statehood.

## Objective: Breaking the "Village Grouping" Trauma
Sailo realized that the **[[Village_Grouping]]** policy had destroyed the soul of the Mizo village. His Unity Mission focused on rebuilding the social capital of the communities. He traveled to the most remote corners of the **[[Mamit_District]]** and **[[Lunglei_District]]**, hosting "Town Hall" style meetings where civilians could voice their grievances against both the underground and the security forces.

## The People's Conference (PC)
The Mission eventually evolved into the **People's Conference** political party. Unlike other parties that focused purely on ethnic nationalism, the PC focused on **[[Brigadier_Sailo_Clean_Politics]]**. They argued that without internal unity and administrative transparency, the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]** would only be a piece of paper.

## Impact on Modern Governance
The Unity Mission's emphasis on "bottom-up" governance is a primary reason why Mizoram's **[[Governance_Overview]]** is so decentralized today. It set the precedent for the **[[Loka_Ayukta_Mizoram]]** and other accountability mechanisms. Sailo's mission proved that a military man could lead a movement for civil peace.
