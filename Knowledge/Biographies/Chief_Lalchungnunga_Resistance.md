# Chief Lalchungnunga: Tactics of Resistance

**Tags:** #history #biography #warfare

**Chief Lalchungnunga's** resistance against British forces in the late 19th century was characterized by a sophisticated understanding of jungle guerrilla tactics.

## Defensive Fortifications
Lalchungnunga was known for selecting village sites that were geographically inaccessible to British artillery. He heavily utilized **[[Mizo_Traditional_Fortifications_Kulh]]**, which involved complex bamboo spikes and hidden pitfalls around the village perimeter. These defenses made the **[[Lushai_Expeditions]]** extremely costly for the invading forces.

## Ambush Strategies
His warriors did not engage in open-set battles. Instead, they used the "hit-and-run" strategy, attacking British coolie lines and supply columns in the narrow passes of the North Lushai Hills. This forced the British to maintain a much higher soldier-to-supply ratio than they had anticipated.

## Diplomacy of Defiant Chiefs
Lalchungnunga also maintained a secret network of communications with the southern **[[Sailo_Chiefs]]**, coordinating their resistance so that the British had to fight on two fronts. This tactical coordination is a legendary part of pre-colonial **[[Mizo_History_Overview]]**.
