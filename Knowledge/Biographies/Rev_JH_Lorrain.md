# Rev. J.H. Lorrain — Pu Buanga (1873–1944)

**Tags:** #biography #missionary #history

**James Herbert Lorrain**, known affectionately to the Mizo as **Pu Buanga** (the Senior Foreign Friend), arrived in [[Aizawl_District|Aizawl]] on 11 January 1894, alongside his colleague F.W. Savidge, representing the Arthington Aborigines Mission.

## Creating the Mizo Script
Within months of arrival, Lorrain systematically documented the phonology of the Mizo language and created a romanised alphabet that remains in use essentially unchanged today. He compiled:
- The first **Mizo primer** (1898).
- The first **Mizo–English dictionary** (1898).
- Early translations of the Gospel.

## Long Service
Lorrain worked in Mizoram for over 40 years across multiple stays. He was fluent in Mizo and is regarded as one of the most important figures in Mizo history. The [[Mizo_Alphabet|script he devised]] gave the Mizo people access to literacy, literature, and self-expression that accelerated their social transformation.
