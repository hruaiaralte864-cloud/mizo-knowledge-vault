# Chief Lalchungnunga (Sailo Clan)



**Chief Lalchungnunga** was a powerful Mizo chief of the Sailo clan who ruled in the northern Lushai Hills during the late 19th century. He is best known for his fierce resistance against the British military expeditions that sought to annex the Mizo hills.

## Leadership and Influence
As a prominent chief, Lalchungnunga controlled several large villages and was respected for his military strategy and diplomacy among other clans. His rule was characterized by the traditional **[[Lal_System]]**, where he held supreme authority over his subjects but governed with a council of elders (**[[Lal_Council_of_Elders]]**).

## Resistance against the British
During the **[[Lushai_Expeditions]]**, Lalchungnunga was one of the primary leaders of the "North Lushai" resistance. He famously refused to pay tributes to the British authorities at Silchar, leading to several skirmishes. His warriors were skilled in guerrilla warfare, utilizing the dense jungle terrain to harass British supply lines.

## Legacy
Lalchungnunga's resistance is a significant chapter in **[[Mizo_History_Overview]]**. Though the hills were eventually annexed in 1891, he is remembered as a symbol of Mizo independence and the strength of the pre-colonial Sailo dynasty.
