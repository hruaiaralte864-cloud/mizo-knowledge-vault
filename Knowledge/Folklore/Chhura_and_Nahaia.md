# Chhura and Nahaia — The Classic Mizo Fable Duo



**Chhura** and **Nahaia** form the Mizo people's most beloved comedic duo — appearing in dozens of interrelated oral folktales.

## Chhura
Chhura is a man of almost supernatural strength and enormous appetite, but hopelessly simple-minded and literal in understanding. He follows instructions with rigid exactness — with disastrous, hilarious results.

*Example:* Asked to "keep an eye on the house" while the family leaves, Chhura gouges out an eye and leaves it sitting on the doorstep.

## Nahaia
Nahaia is physically weak, chronically lazy, and pathologically cunning. He survives entirely on his wits, usually by manipulating Chhura into doing all the hard work while Nahaia takes credit or steals the reward.

## Cultural Function
The Chhura-Nahaia tales are the Mizo equivalent of Aesop's Fables — entertaining on the surface while encoding deep moral lessons about the dangers of blind literalism, the value of wisdom over brute strength, and the ultimately self-defeating nature of laziness and dishonesty.
