# Pawla — The Mizo Trickster Hero

**Tags:** #folklore #mythology

**Pawla** is a folk hero of a different flavour from the strong-but-foolish [[Chhura_and_Nahaia|Chhura]] — he is a quick-witted young man who uses clever thinking to escape impossible situations, defeat physically superior opponents, and right social wrongs.

## Typical Pawla Stories
Pawla tales often involve:
- A young man of humble background who outwits a powerful chief or a supernatural entity.
- Clever wordplay and misdirection that allows him to escape execution or slavery.
- A test of wit set by a king, where Pawla solves the unsolvable riddle by thinking laterally.

## Cultural Role
Pawla represents the Mizo ideal that cleverness and quick thinking are survival virtues as critical as physical strength (represented by [[Chhura_and_Nahaia|Chhura]]). Together, Chhura, Nahaia, and Pawla form a triangular model of human nature in Mizo folk ethics.
