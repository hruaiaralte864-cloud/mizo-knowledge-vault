# Tualvungi and Zawlpala — The Great Mizo Romance

**Tags:** #folklore #romance

The love story of **Tualvungi** and **Zawlpala** is the definitive great romance of Mizo literary culture — carrying the same cultural weight as Romeo and Juliet.

## The Tragedy
Tualvungi was the most beautiful woman in the region. She and the handsome Zawlpala were deeply, happily married. 

A powerful chief, **Phuntiha**, became obsessed with Tualvungi. He used political manipulation and enormous wealth to trick the naive Zawlpala — who agreed under extreme duress to an exorbitant bride price return — allowing Phuntiha to legally take Tualvungi away.

Both Tualvungi and Zawlpala were destroyed by the separation. Zawlpala died of heartbreak. When Tualvungi heard the news, she tricked Phuntiha, escaped to Zawlpala's village, and died by suicide at his grave so their souls could travel together to [[Pialral]].

## The Yellow Butterflies
Legend holds that their souls transformed into a pair of yellow butterflies (*Chhiarthlang*) that are always seen flying together, inseparably. When a pair of yellow butterflies is spotted together, the Mizo say Tualvungi and Zawlpala are near.
