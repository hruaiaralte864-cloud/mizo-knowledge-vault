# Mauruangi — The Mizo Cinderella



**Mauruangi** is the Mizo people's equivalent of Cinderella — a deeply beloved tale of a mistreated girl whose goodness ultimately triumphs.

## The Story
Mauruangi was a beautiful, gentle girl who lost her mother young. Her stepmother despised her and forced her to do all the heaviest work — fetching water from the river, gathering firewood, pounding rice — while her stepsisters did nothing.

Despite constant abuse, Mauruangi remained kind and uncomplaining, embodying [[Tlawmngaihna]].

A handsome young man from a neighbouring village fell deeply in love with her goodness more than her beauty. With the help of a magical intervention (in various versions: a fairy, a supernatural bird, or the spirit of her dead mother), Mauruangi's true qualities were made visible to all, her stepmother's cruelty was exposed, and she was elevated to a position of happiness and respect.
