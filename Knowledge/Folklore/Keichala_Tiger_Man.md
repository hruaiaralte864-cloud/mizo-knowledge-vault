# Keichala (The Tiger-Man)

**Tags:** #folklore

The legend of the **Kei-mi** (Tiger-person) is a recurring theme in Mizo folklore, with **Keichala** being the most famous.

## Shape-shifting
Keichala was said to be a man who could transform into a tiger. These stories often dealt with themes of duality, the thin line between the human village and the wild jungle, and the consequences of breaking social taboos. These myths reflected the real-world danger and respect that Mizos had for the tigers in their forests.
