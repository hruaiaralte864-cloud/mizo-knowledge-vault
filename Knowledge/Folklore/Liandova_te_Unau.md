# Liandova te Unau — The Brothers' Fortune



A foundational Mizo moral fable about two orphaned brothers whose poverty turned to wealth through unwavering honesty and brotherly love.

## The Story
**Liandova** and his younger brother **Tuaisiala** were the poorest people in their village — orphaned young, mocked by neighbors, and surviving on jungle foraging.

One day deep in the forest, they discovered a massive wounded Python — the king of all serpents. Despite being hungry themselves, they nursed the python with whatever meager food they had, treating its wounds with jungle herbs.

As reward, the serpent king revealed a hidden treasure beneath a boulder and gave them the ability to understand the language of birds and animals. Using this gift (and maintaining their characteristic humility and honesty), they gradually rose to wealth and status. Liandova won the heart of the Chief's daughter — not through wealth or trickery but through his character.

## Moral
Honest kindness extended even to strangers and animals will ultimately be repaid with abundance.
