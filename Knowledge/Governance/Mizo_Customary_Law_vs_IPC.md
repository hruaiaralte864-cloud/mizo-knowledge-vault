# Mizo Customary Law vs. Indian Penal Code (IPC)

**Tags:** #governance #law #society

The coexistence of **Mizo Customary Law** and the **Indian Penal Code (IPC)** is a unique feature of the state’s legal system, protected under **[[Article_371G_Analysis]]**.

## The Jurisdictional Split
- **Civil Matters**: Most civil disputes, especially those involving marriage (**[[Mizo_Marriage_Gifts_Thul]]**), inheritance, and divorce, are handled according to Customary Law. The Village Council acts as the primary adjudicator.
- **Criminal Matters**: Serious crimes like murder or grand theft are prosecuted under the IPC by the regular state judiciary. However, even in these cases, the "Compensation Logic" of Mizo tradition can influence sentencing.

## Rule of Compensation (Vawk Thuna)
A core tenet of Customary Law is "Restoration over Retribution." In traditional disputes, the focus is on a symbolic fine or communal service (*Hnatlang*), rather than jail time. This is a direct application of the **[[Tlawmngaihna]]** code—seeking to heal the rift in the community rather than just punish the individual.

## Modern Codification
The **[[Mizo_Customary_Law_Registry]]** has meticulously documented these traditions to ensure they are compatible with modern human rights standards. This ongoing dialogue between "Ancient Ethics" and "Modern Law" is a key part of the **[[Mizoram_State_Planning_Board]]** social goals.
