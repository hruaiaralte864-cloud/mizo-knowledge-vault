# Traditional Grievance Redressal: The Penalty System



Mizo society has always been "Highly Regularized." Even before written laws, the village used a sophisticated system of social penalties to maintain order.

## The "Sawk" (Social Fine)
Traditional Mizo law did not rely on prisons. Instead, it used the **Sawk**—a fine paid in livestock (mostly pigs) or community service. If a person committed a crime, the **[[Mizoram_Village_Councils]]** (historical Council of Elders) would decide the fine, which was then consumed by the village in a peace-making feast.

## Enforcing Hnatlang
The **[[Hnatlang]]** (community work) system is mandatory. Those who fail to show up without a valid medical reason must pay a small fine (*Hnatlang-phiah*). This historical penalty ensures 100% participation in village construction or disaster relief, a system that underpins the **[[The_Haupu_Landslide_Disaster]]** response.

## Modern Evolution
This "Social Policing" remains powerful. While modern crimes go to the **[[Guwahati_High_Court_Aizawl_Bench]]**, minor local disputes are still settled by the **[[YMA_Central_Leadership]]** or Village Councils using these ancient peer-pressure and reconciliation models.
