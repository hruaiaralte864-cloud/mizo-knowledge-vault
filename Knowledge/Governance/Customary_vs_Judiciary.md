# Mizo Customary Law vs. Modern Judiciary

Mizoram operates under the **Sixth Schedule of the Indian Constitution**, which provides a unique legal autonomy. The tension and synergy between *Mizo Duhdan* (Traditional Customs) and modern Indian Law is a critical area of governance research.

## ⚖️ The Village Council Court
The Village Council (VC) is the grassroots judicial authority.
- **Jurisdiction**: Petty crimes, land disputes, and social grievances within the village.
- **Principles**: Focus on restoration and community harmony (*Hnatlang* spirit) rather than purely punitive measures.

## 👰 Bride Price & Inheritance (Hmeichhe Dikna)
- **Customary Law**: The payment of *Man* (Bride Price) remains an essential cultural rite.
- **Inheritance**: Traditionally, the youngest son (*Zatana*) inherited the father's property. 
- **Modern Change**: The *Mizoram Mizo Marriage, Divorce and Inheritance of Property Act, 2014* significantly reformed these customs, allowing women to inherit property under specific conditions—marking a major shift toward gender equity.

## 🌲 Land Tenure Systems
- **Traditional**: Land was communal, governed by the Chief (*Lal*).
- **Modern**: The transition to individual land titles (*Patta*) has created a complex intersection where traditional *Jhum* (shifting cultivation) rights clash with modern commercial land ownership.

## 🛡️ Protection of Identity
Specific laws like the **Inner Line Permit (ILP)** are judicial extensions of the Mizo need to protect their demographic and cultural integrity from outside influence.
