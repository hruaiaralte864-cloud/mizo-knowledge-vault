# Land Revenue and Settlement in Mizoram

**Tags:** #governance #law #economy

The **Land Revenue and Settlement** system in Mizoram is a complex blend of traditional communal ownership and modern individual property rights, reflecting the state’s unique constitutional status.

## From Communal to Individual Rights
Traditionally, land was owned by the village community and allotted by the **[[Lal_System]]** (Chiefs) for Jhumming. Post-independence, the state introduced "Land Settlement" certificates, allowing for individual ownership. This transition has been crucial for enabling farmers to use land as collateral for loans from the **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]**.

## Regulatory Environment
The system is governed by the *Mizoram Land Holding and Settlement Act*. It ensures that land remains in the hands of the "indigenous Mizo people," preventing alien ownership as per the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]** protections. This makes land the primary asset for the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** goals.

## Digitization and Modernization
As part of the **[[Governance_Overview]]** initiatives, the state is currently using GIS mapping to digitize all land records. This reduces boundary disputes and facilitates the "Handholding Scheme" by providing accurate data on farm locations and sizes.
