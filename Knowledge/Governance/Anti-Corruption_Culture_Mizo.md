# Anti-Corruption Culture in Mizoram



Mizoram has a distinctive "Anti-Corruption Culture" that is driven as much by civil society vigilance as it is by formal state institutions. This culture is a legacy of both traditional ethics and early political reformism.

## Institutional Framework
The state has established the **[[Loka_Ayukta_Mizoram]]**, an independent body with the power to investigate and prosecute corruption at the highest levels. This is supported by the **[[Mizoram_State_Legal_Services_Authority]]**, which provides legal literacy to citizens, empowering them to use the Right to Information (RTI) effectively.

## Civil Society Watchdogs
The **[[Young_Mizo_Association_YMA]]** and the PRISM (People's Right to Information and Development Society of Mizoram) act as powerful watchdogs. They frequently conduct "social audits" of government projects, ensuring that funds allocated in the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]** are spent transparently.

## Ethical Roots
The zero-tolerance for corruption is rooted in the **[[Zawlbuk]]** spirit of transparency and the **[[Brigadier_Sailo_Clean_Politics]]** movement of the 1970s. This has led to Mizoram consistently ranking high on transparency indices compared to other Indian states.
