# Humanitarian Diplomacy: The Chin-Kuki Modern Reality



Mizoram occupies a unique geopolitical position as a "Sanctuary State" for ethnic kin (Chin-Kuki groups) fleeing conflict in neighboring Myanmar and Manipur.

## The Concept of "Ethnic Kinship"
Mizoram's policy toward refugees is driven by the ancient **[[Zohnahthlak]]** identity. Despite national-level complexities, the Mizo people and government have consistently provided food, shelter, and education to those fleeing violence, viewing it as a modern exercise of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**.

## State-Level Management
The **[[Mizoram_Police_Commandos]]** and local village councils coordinate to maintain peace and security. The state has integrated refugee children into local schools, ensuring a humanitarian base while balancing the limited resources of the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**.

## Geopolitical Balancing
Mizoram acts as a bridge for "Track-II Diplomacy," maintaining state stability while advocating for the human rights of the Chin people. This sensitive role is critical to the success of the **[[Act_East_Policy_Strategic_Vision]]** and is a major focus of the **[[Mizoram_2050_Projections]]**.
