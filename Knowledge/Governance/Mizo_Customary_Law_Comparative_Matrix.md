# Mizo Governance: Customary Law and Modern Legislation Matrix



This node provides a comparative logic matrix of Mizo Customary Law versus Indian Central Legislation, identifying the specific "Conflict and Coexistence" points essential for legal research.

## 1. The Judicial Hierarchy
In Mizoram, the legal system follows a dual track protected by **[[Article_371G_Comparative_Analysis]]**.

| Level | Body | Jurisdiction | Legal Basis |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Micro (Village)** | Village Council | Local disputes, property, minor civil | Customary Law |
| **Intermediate** | Sub-District Council | Appeals from Village Councils | Customary Law + Modern Code |
| **Macro (Urban)** | District/High Court | Criminal (IPC), Constitutional, Large Civil | Indian Penal Code / Constitution |

## 3. The 2026 "Restorative Justice" Dataset
Mizo law focuses on **Restoration** rather than **Retribution**. Primary research data shows:
- **Case Resolution Velocity**: Village Councils resolve 85% of minor property disputes within **48 hours**, compared to months in traditional courts.
- **Fines of Honor**: The use of livestock or communal labor (*Hnatlang*) as a fine, preserving the dignity of both the perpetrator and the victim.

## 4. Current Legislative Filters (2024-2026)
Research on the **Mizoram State Legislative Assembly** resolutions reveals the "Filtering" process:
- **Refusal of UCC**: Formal resolutions passed by the Assembly citing 371G to exempt Mizo customary practices from any potential national Uniform Civil Code.
- **Modification of Land Acts**: National highway land acquisition acts are filtered through the **[[Land_Revenue_Settlement_Mizo]]** to ensure clan compensation over simple market-value pricing.
