# Sixth_Schedule: The Shield of Autonomy

**Tags:** #governance #constitutional #history

The **Sixth_Schedule** of the Indian Constitution provides for the administration of tribal areas in North and Northeast India. In Mizoram, it is the legal basis for the **[[The_Three_Autonomous_District_Councils]]**.

## Administrative Autonomy
Unlike the **[[Fifth_Schedule]]** which focuses on state-led development for tribes, the Sixth_Schedule grants "Legislative and Judicial Autonomy" to the tribal bodies themselves. This means that the ADCs have their own budgets and can enact laws on matters like primary education and community markets without State Assembly interference.

## Cultural Preservation
The Schedule allows the **[[Siaha_District]]** and **[[Lawngtlai_District]]** to preserve their specific linguistic identities (Mara and Lai). It ensures that developmental projects like the **[[Kaladan_Multi-Modal_Transit_Transport_Project]]** provide benefits directly to the local councils.

## Link to Statehood
The Sixth_Schedule was a critical pre-condition for many rebel groups to join the mainstream. It ensures that "Statehood" does not mean "Standardization," allowing for a diverse and multi-ethnic **[[Mizoram_Political_History]]**.
