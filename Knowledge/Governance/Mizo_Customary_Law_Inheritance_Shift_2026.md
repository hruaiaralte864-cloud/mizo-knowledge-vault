# Mizo Customary Law: The 2026 Inheritance Evolution



Modern Mizo society is undergoing a tectonic legal shift regarding gender equality and property inheritance.

## Breaking the Primogeniture
Historically, Mizo customary law favored the youngest son (*Fatawp*) for inheritance. However, recent legal movements and amendments enforced through the **[[Mizoram_State_Governance_Model]]** and championed by the **[[MHIP_Mizo_Hmeichhe_Insuikhawm_Pawl]]** (Mizo Women's Federation) allow for daughters to inherit property.

## Legislative Impact
These changes are protected under the autonomy of **[[Article_371G]]**, allowing the state to codify laws that adapt tradition to modern human rights standards without external interference.

## Social Reconciliation
This evolution reflects the transition of the **[[Traditional_Grievance_Redressal_Mizo]]** system from a purely patriarchal structure to a inclusive modern democracy.
