# Mizoram Rural Bank (MRB)



The **Mizoram Rural_Bank (MRB)** is the largest regional rural bank in the state, serving as the financial backbone for the rural Mizo economy. Established in 1983, it is sponsored by the State Bank of India.

## Reach and Penetration
The MRB has the most extensive network of branches in Mizoram, reaching remote villages where private banks do not operate. It is the primary vehicle for implementing the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** (Handholding Scheme), as it handles the disbursement of grants and micro-loans to farmers and artisans.

## Impact on Financial Inclusion
Under the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**, the MRB has integrated UPI and mobile banking services to reach the agrarian population. They provide specialized "Kisan Credit Cards" (KCC) for **[[Mizo_Cardamom_Agriculture]]** and **[[Zo_Zial_Cultivation_History]]**, enabling farmers to bypass informal moneylenders.

## Social Role
The bank also partners with the **[[Mizoram_Youth_Commission_MYC]]** to provide educational loans for students. Its role in stabilizing the rural economy during the **[[Mautam_Famine_Cycle]]** was historically significant, providing the necessary credit to rebuild livelihoods after the bamboo famine.
