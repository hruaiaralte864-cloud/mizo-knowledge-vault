# The Autonomous District Councils (ADCs) of Mizoram

**Tags:** #governance #politics #sixth_schedule

Mizoram is home to three **Autonomous District Councils** established under the **[[Sixth_Schedule]]** of the Constitution of India. These bodies provide self-governance to the minority ethnic groups in the southern and western hills.

## 1. Lai Autonomous District Council (LADC)
- **Headquarters**: Lawngtlai (see **[[Lawngtlai_Lai_Council_House]]**).
- **People**: Primarily the Lai people.
- **Focus**: Cultural preservation and local infrastructure development in the southern corridor, especially with the **[[Kaladan_Multi-Modal_Transit_Transport_Project]]** passing through its territory.

## 2. Mara Autonomous District Council (MADC)
- **Headquarters**: Siaha (see **[[Siaha_Mara_Council_House]]**).
- **People**: The Mara people (formerly known as Shendu).
- **Focus**: Management of the **[[Palak_Dil_Lake]]** tourism and traditional Mara education systems.

## 3. Chakma Autonomous District Council (CADC)
- **Headquarters**: Kamalanagar.
- **People**: The Chakma people.
- **Focus**: Development along the **[[Mamit_Boundary_Relations]]** and the Indo-Bangladesh border.

## Powers and Functions
Each council has the power to make laws on land, forests, village administration, and social customs. They are funded directly by the **[[North_Eastern_Council_NEC_Role]]** and the state government, serving as a pillar of Mizoram's decentralized **[[Governance_Overview]]**.
