# Mizoram Politics & Governance

Mizoram is often cited as a "Peace Island" in Northeast India. Its political structure is a unique fusion of modern parliamentary democracy and tribal autonomy.

## 🏛️ Constitutional Framework (The 6th Schedule)
Mizoram's governance is underpinned by **Article 371G** of the Indian Constitution, which provides that no Act of Parliament in respect of Mizo religious or social practices, customary law, and land ownership shall apply unless the State Legislative Assembly decides so.
- **District Councils**: Autonomous District Councils (ADCs) exist for the Mara, Lai, and Chakma ethnic minorities, providing them with legislative and executive powers over local matters.

## 🕊️ Post-Accord Political Landscape (1986 - Present)
- **Birth of the State**: Mizoram transitioned from a Union Territory to a full state in 1987 following the Peace Accord.
- **Regional Powers**: The **Mizo National Front (MNF)** and the **Zoram People's Movement (ZPM)** are the dominant regional forces, focusing on Mizo identity and nationalism.
- **National Presence**: Major national parties like the INC and BJP have a presence, but the political heart of the state remains intensely regional.

## 🤝 The Role of NGOs in Governance
Uniquely, civil society organizations like the **Central Young Mizo Association (CYMA)** and **Mizo Hmeichhe Insuikhawm Pawl (MHIP)** act as "moral governors." They often collaborate with the state government on social issues (e.g., drug control, disaster relief), creating a "participatory democracy" model.
