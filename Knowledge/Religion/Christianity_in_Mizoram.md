# Christianity in Mizoram

**Tags:** #religion #society #history

Today approximately **87% of Mizoram's population is Christian**, making it one of the most intensely Christian states in India. The majority are **Protestants** — predominantly Presbyterian in the north and Baptist in the south.

## Spread of Christianity
The [[Missionary_Arrival|Welsh and English missionaries]] who arrived in 1894 found a people who were deeply spiritually inclined but living under intense fear of [[Ramhuai_Spirits|evil spirits]]. The gospel's message of a loving, accessible God who conquered fear transformed Mizo society. Christianity spread with astonishing speed — within a single generation, entire villages converted.

## The Church's Role in Modern Society
- **[[Young_Mizo_Association]]** and church networks run Mizoram's social welfare architecture.
- Sundays are completely silent across the state — no commerce, no vehicles, no sports.
- The Church lobby drives the state's **prohibition policy on alcohol**.
- Presbyterian synod offices are architecturally and politically dominant in Aizawl.
