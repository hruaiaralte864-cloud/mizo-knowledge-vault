# Mizo Funeral Customs



Death in Mizo society triggers the most powerful demonstration of [[Tlawmngaihna]] — the entire community mobilises immediately and unconditionally.

## Immediate Response
The moment a death is announced (traditionally by a church bell toll, today also via WhatsApp), work entirely stops in the neighbourhood. Neighbours of both sexes immediately begin:
- Men: sawing and building the wooden coffin, digging the grave.
- Women: cooking enormous quantities of rice, pork, and vegetables to feed all who come.
- Young men: running errands, setting up canopies, arranging seating.

## The Wake (*Mitthi Piak*)
The body is kept at home for one night. The entire community — hundreds of people — attend through the night, singing hymns without pause. This is both a spiritual act and a community act of solidarity.

## Burial and Feast
The next morning, after a church service, a grand communal feast is held. The family provides rice and meat for the entire community regardless of expense. Refusing to host this feast would be devastating to family honour. The [[Young_Mizo_Association]] often assists families who cannot afford the costs.
