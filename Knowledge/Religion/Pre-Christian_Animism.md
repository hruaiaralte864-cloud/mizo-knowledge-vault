# Pre-Christian Animism — Mizo Traditional Religion



Before 1894, the Mizo people practised a form of fear-based animism with the following core structure:

## Pathian — The Supreme Creator
**Pathian** (derived from an older Kuki-Chin theological concept) was the supreme, all-creating, invisible God. He was considered benevolent but utterly transcendent and uninvolved in human daily life. Humans could not directly access Pathian.

## Ramhuai — Forest Spirits
Daily life was dominated by intense fear of the **[[Ramhuai_Spirits|Ramhuai]]** — malevolent spirits lurking in dark forests, deep pools, large rocks, and ancient trees. All diseases, nightmares, accidents, and crop failures were attributed to Ramhuai attacks.

## The Bawlpu — Shaman
The **Bawlpu** (shaman/witch doctor) was the specialist who mediated between humans and the spirit world. A Bawlpu would perform animal sacrifices (pigs, chickens, dogs) and elaborate rituals in the forest to appease the Ramhuai and cure the sick.

## Lasi — Forest Fairies
**Lasi** were benevolent female spirits who governed and protected wild animals. Hunters appealed to Lasi before hunts.
