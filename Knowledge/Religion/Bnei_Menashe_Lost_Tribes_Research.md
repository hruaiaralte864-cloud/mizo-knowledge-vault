# Bnei Menashe: The "Lost Tribe" Identity



The **Bnei Menashe** (Sons of Manasseh) refers to a group within the Mizo-Chin-Kuki family who believe they are descendants of one of the "Ten Lost Tribes" of Israel. This movement has significantly influenced modern Mizo religious and political identity.

## Origins of the Movement
While the majority of Mizos are Christian, a movement began in the 1950s led by visionaries like *Mela Chala*, who claimed that the "Chhinlung" (Sinlung) origin of the Mizos was actually a memory of the "Lost Tribe of Manasseh." This led to a gradual "reversion" to Judaism for thousands of families.

## Aliyah and Global Diaspora
Since the 1990s, over 7,000 Bnei Menashe have made **Aliyah** (migrated) to Israel, primarily with the support of organizations like Shavei Israel. This has created a vibrant Mizo diaspora in Israeli cities like Kiryat Arba and Sderot. The **[[Mizo_Diaspora_Israel_Bnei_Menashe]]** contributes significantly to the local economy through remittances and knowledge exchange.

## Genetic and Cultural Debate
While modern DNA studies provide complex and often debated results, the Bnei Menashe movement is primarily an **Identity Movement**. It highlights the "Zo" people's desire to find a global, ancient lineage that predates the colonial and missionary era. This is a primary topic in the **[[Mizo_Cultural_Identity]]** and **[[Zo_Literary_Festival]]**.
