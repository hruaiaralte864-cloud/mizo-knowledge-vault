# The Presbyterian Church of Mizoram (PCM)



The **Presbyterian Church of Mizoram (PCM)**, officially the Synod of the Presbyterian Church of India in Mizoram, is the largest and most powerful religious institution in the state.

## Origins
Founded by the **Welsh Presbyterian Mission** which arrived in 1897, a few years after the initial Arthington Mission of [[Rev_JH_Lorrain|Lorrain and Savidge]]. The Welsh missionaries set up the denominational structure that grew into the PCM.

## Scale and Influence
- Over **400,000 members** — the single largest denomination.
- Operates hundreds of schools, hospitals, and colleges.
- The **Synod headquarters** in Aizawl is architecturally and politically one of the most prominent buildings in the state capital.
- The **Synod Press** publishes textbooks, hymnbooks, and literature that have shaped Mizo literacy and culture for over a century.
- Sunday church attendance in Mizoram is near-universal — even government offices close on Sunday mornings.
