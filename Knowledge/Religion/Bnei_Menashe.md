# Bnei Menashe — The Lost Tribe Claim



The **Bnei Menashe** (Hebrew: "Sons of Manasseh") is a religious-ethnic movement among a segment of Mizo and Kuki people in Northeast India who believe themselves to be descendants of the Manasseh tribe of ancient Israel — one of the Ten Lost Tribes.

## The Belief System
According to their tradition, their ancestors were part of the northern Israelite kingdom deported by the Assyrian Empire around 722 BCE. They gradually migrated eastward through the Middle East, Central Asia, and eventually into the hills of Southeast Asia.

Scholars note that several traditional Mizo oral customs have intriguing parallels with ancient Israelite practice — animal sacrifice, purification rituals, menstrual taboos, and the oral transmission of a creation narrative mentioning a flood.

## Aliyah (Immigration to Israel)
The Bnei Menashe movement is recognised by some Israeli rabbinical authorities. Thousands of Mizo and Kuki Bnei Menashe have made *aliyah* (immigration to Israel), undergo formal conversion to Judaism, and live in Israeli communities, primarily in the West Bank settlements and the Galilee.
