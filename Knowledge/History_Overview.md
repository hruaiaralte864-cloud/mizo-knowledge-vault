# Mizo History: From Chhinlung to Statehood

The history of the Mizo people is a remarkable journey of migration, resistance, and peaceful integration. It is characterized by the transition from independent hill tribes to a modern democratic state within the Indian Union.

## 🏺 The Mythical Origins (Chhinlung)
According to Mizo oral tradition, the ancestors emerged from a cave or rock called **Chhinlung**. 
- **Migration Path**: Ancient Mizos are part of the Tibeto-Burman wave that migrated from China, through the Yalung River, into the Shan State (Myanmar), and eventually into the Khampat region in the Kabaw Valley.
- **Settlement in Chin Hills**: Around the 16th century, the Mizos settled in the Chin Hills before crossing the Tiau River into present-day Mizoram in the 18th century.

## ⚔️ The Colonial Encounter (1891 - 1947)
- **The Blackwood's Expedition**: After series of raids by Mizo chiefs on British tea plantations, the British launched military expeditions.
- **Annexation**: By 1891, the Lushai Hills were formally annexed into the British Empire.
- **Christianity**: Missionaries (Savidge and Lorrain in 1894) introduced the Latin script, transforming the oral language into a written one and triggering a massive social overhaul.

## 🕊️ The Struggle & Peace (1966 - 1986)
- **1966 Uprising**: Led by the **Mizo National Front (MNF)** under Pu Laldenga, a 20-year insurgency began following the "Mautam" famine neglect.
- **Mizo Peace Accord (1986)**: Signed between the Government of India and the MNF, it is globally recognized as one of the most successful peace treaties.
- **Statehood**: On February 20, 1987, Mizoram became the 23rd state of India.
