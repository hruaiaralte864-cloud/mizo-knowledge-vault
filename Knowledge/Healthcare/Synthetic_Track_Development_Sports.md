# Synthetic Track Development and Sports Health



Mizoram has embarked on a massive infrastructure project to install "High-Performance Synthetic Tracks" across every district, a move aimed at enhancing the physical health and professional prospects of the Mizo youth.

## Technical Implementation
The development, funded primarily through the **[[North_Eastern_Council_NEC_Role]]** and the state’s **"Handholding" funds**, uses FIFA-approved turf and IAAF-standard athletic tracks. These tracks are designed to withstand the heavy Mizoram rains, which previously rendered natural grass pitches unusable for half the year.

## Sports Science and Health
Specialized centers like the **[[Regional_Sports_Training_Centre_Aizawl]]** use these tracks to conduct biomechanical assessments of athletes like **[[Jeremy_Lalrinnunga_Training]]**. This focus on infrastructure is linked to a broader healthcare goal: reducing substance abuse among the youth by providing world-class sporting outlets.

## Economic Impact
The presence of these tracks allows Mizoram to host national-level tournaments, boosting the **[[Mizoram_Tourism_Development_Authority]]** revenue. It is a key pillar of the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** focus on human capital development.
