# Bamboo — The Tree of Mizo Life

**Tags:** #culture #economy #nature

Bamboo (*Rawt*) is not merely a plant in Mizoram — it is the structural foundation of traditional Mizo civilisation and a major ongoing component of the modern economy.

## Traditional Uses
Almost every part of traditional Mizo life relied on bamboo:
- **Architecture:** Walls, floors, roofs, furniture — see [[Traditional_Mizo_Architecture]].
- **Tools:** Farming implements, water carriers, cooking utensils.
- **Weapons:** Spears, bows, defensive stockades.
- **Food:** Young bamboo shoots (*Kawlbuk*) are a key ingredient, eaten fresh or fermented.

## The Mautam Curse
The same bamboo that sustains Mizo life also periodically destroys it. The mass-flowering (*Mautam*) event — see [[Mautam_Famine_1959]] — triggers rat plagues that devastate harvests.

## Modern Bamboo Industry
Mizoram has over 3,000 sq km of natural bamboo forests — the densest concentration in India. The government has actively promoted bamboo handicrafts (baskets, furniture, flooring) and agro-industries (bamboo shoots processing) as economic development vectors.
