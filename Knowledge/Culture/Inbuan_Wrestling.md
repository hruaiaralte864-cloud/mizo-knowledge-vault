# Inbuan Wrestling

**Tags:** #culture #sport #tradition

**Inbuan** is the traditional wrestling form of the Mizo people, believed to have originated in the village of **Dungtlang** in what is now the Lunglei district during the early 19th century.

## Rules
Inbuan has unusually strict rules that distinguish it from most global wrestling traditions:
- Wrestlers must **not bend their knees** at any point — a fall or knee-touch is an instant loss.
- They must maintain a constant **tight grip** on each other's waist belt (*lungloh*).
- They cannot step **outside a small circle** drawn in the ground.
- No kicking, tripping with legs, or releasing and re-gripping.

## Cultural Role
Inbuan was performed during festivals and inter-village competitions. Exceptional wrestlers earned the title of *Bawitu* (Champion) and were accorded immense community respect, comparable to earning the [[Thangchhuah]] honour.

Today Inbuan is practised as a recreational sport and features prominently at [[Chapchar_Kut]] festivals.
