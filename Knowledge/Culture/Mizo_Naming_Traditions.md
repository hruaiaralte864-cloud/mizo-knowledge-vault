# Mizo Naming Traditions

**Tags:** #culture #language #society

Mizo naming customs are complex, poetic, and deeply culturally encoded.

## Structure
Traditional Mizo names are typically single compound words. They often contain:
- **Pu / Pi / Nu / Pa** — terms of seniority embedded in names.
- **Lal** — meaning chief/lord, indicating high-status lineage.
- **Mawia / Mawii** — beauty or grace.
- **Hruaia / Hruaii** — wisdom or leadership.
- **Thanga / Thangi** — gold / precious.
- **Zuala / Zuali** — linked to the concept of fortune.

## Naming Ceremony
A newborn is given a 'milk name' (*thinlung hming*) immediately at birth, often chosen by a grandmother. A permanent, considered name (*hming dik*) is typically given within days, chosen by the father or a respected elder, often themed on a meaningful event at the time of birth.

## Christian Influence
After [[Missionary_Arrival|Christian conversion]], it became extremely common to pair a traditional Mizo given name with a Christian name — either a Biblical name or a Welsh/English name taken from hymn-books. Example: *Lalthanzara James* or *Vanlalruati Mary*.
