# Hlado — Traditional Mizo Song Tradition



**Hlado** is the collective term for the traditional Mizo genre of antiphonal (call-and-response) folk songs. They were historically performed during communal work parties (*Ramri*), at festivals, and as courtship songs.

## Types of Hlado
- **Tlanglam Hlado:** Songs sung in the fields during communal farming work.
- **Hlabu:** Poetic improvised songs used as a form of verbal courtship. Young men and women would engage in sung poetic duels during festivals, composing verses on the spot.
- **Khawnglung Hlado:** Accompanied by the traditional bamboo-string instrument (*Khawnglung*).

## Traditional Instruments
- **Khuang:** The two-headed Mizo drum, played at festivals.
- **Darkhuang:** A large brass gong struck rhythmically.
- **Tumte:** A bamboo flute.
- **Dar:** Brass cymbals used in ceremonial contexts.
