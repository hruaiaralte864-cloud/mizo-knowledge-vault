# Man — The Mizo Bride Price



In Mizo society, when a man marries, the groom's family pays a **bride price** known as **Man** to the bride's family. This is the inverse of the South Asian dowry system.

## Structure of the Man
The Man is not a simple lump sum but is precisely divided among different relatives, binding them each with a stake in the marriage's stability:

| Component | Recipient | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Manpui** | Bride's father/parents | Main payment |
| **Pusum** | Maternal grandfather | Recognition of lineage |
| **Palal** | Bride's favourite uncle | Guardian acknowledgement |
| **Ni-ar** | Paternal aunt | Extended family bond |

## Divorce Obligations
If the marriage breaks down due to the wife's fault, portions of the Man must be returned. If due to the husband's fault, no return is required. This created legal accountability distributed across the entire extended family.
