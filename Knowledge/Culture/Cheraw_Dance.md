# Cheraw — The Bamboo Dance

**Tags:** #culture #dance #art

The **Cheraw** is the most iconic and globally recognised traditional dance of the Mizo people, performed primarily during [[Chapchar_Kut]] and state cultural events.

## How It Works
Pairs of men sit on the ground holding long bamboo poles horizontally. They clap the poles together rhythmically, while female dancers (*Hmeichhe*) step in and out between the clapping poles with extraordinary precision, grace, and footwork — weaving between the poles without being snapped.

The dance demands acute timing from both the pole operators and the dancers. Colourful traditional *[[Puan]]* wraps and flowers in the hair are essential to the costume.

## Origin
Legend holds that Cheraw was originally performed to console the soul of a woman who died in childbirth, keeping her spirit happily entertained as it prepared to depart for the afterlife.
