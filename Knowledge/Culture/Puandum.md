# Puandum



**Puandum** is a deep black or dark blue Puan with red and green stripes. It carries heavy ritual significance in Mizo life and death.

## The Marriage Gift
Traditionally, a Mizo bride must bring a Puandum to her new home. It was her duty to weave it herself.

## The shroud
The Puandum was also used as a shroud to wrap the body of a deceased family member. This duality — appearing at both the beginning of a family life and at its end — makes the Puandum the most emotionally significant textile in Mizo culture.
