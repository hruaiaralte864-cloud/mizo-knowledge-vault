# Modern Puan: The Handloom Revival



The **Mizo Puan** has undergone a significant "modernist revival" in the last decade, transitioning from a traditional garment to a piece of high-fashion contemporary attire while preserving ancient weaving techniques.

## Innovation in Patterns
Modern weavers in the **[[Aizawl_District]]** are experimenting with the traditional **Puanchei** and **Kawrchei** patterns by introducing new color palettes and lighter yarns. Designers are incorporating traditional motifs like the *Disul* (meandering line) and *Sakei-zang-zia* (tiger stripes) into modern jackets, bags, and Western-style skirts.

## Government Support
The **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]** has allocated specific funds for "Handloom Clusters." These clusters provide weavers with modern looms and access to premium cotton and silk threads. The **[[Mizoram_Rural_Bank]]** offers micro-credit to women-led self-help groups (SHGs) to scale their production for national and international exhibitions.

## Social Impact
The handloom industry is the second-largest employer in rural Mizoram after agriculture. The revival is a core part of the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** focus on "vibrant rural industries." It empowers women by preserving the **[[Mizo_Handicrafts_Textiles]]** heritage while providing a sustainable livelihood.
