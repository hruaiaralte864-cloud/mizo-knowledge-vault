# Mizo Cuisine



Mizo cuisine is characterised by simplicity, strong fermented flavours, minimal spice, and a heavy reliance on pork, chicken, rice, and jungle greens.

## Staple Dishes

**Bai**
The national dish of Mizoram. A thin stew made by boiling leafy green vegetables (*sag*) together with pork fat or pork bones, *sawhchiar* (fermented pork), and sometimes dried bamboo shoots. Eaten with sticky rice.

**Sawhchiar**
Fermented, salted boiled pork — the cornerstone ingredient of Mizo cooking. Made by boiling pork, salting it heavily, and leaving it to ferment. Adds a deeply pungent, savoury umami base to dishes.

**Vawksa Rep (Smoked Pork)**
Pork that is slowly smoked over a wood fire for several days until completely desiccated. Has an intense smoky flavour and keeps for months without refrigeration — crucial in the remote hills.

**Chhangban**
Flat bread made from rice flour, steamed in banana leaf parcels.

**Zu (Rice Beer)**
Traditional fermented rice or maize brew — see [[Zu]].

## Food Culture
Food sharing (*Sa-huai* — community meat distribution) is a central practice. At any major event, meat is distributed to every household in the community regardless of wealth.
