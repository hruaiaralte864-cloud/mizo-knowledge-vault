# Lianchhiari's Poetry: The Song of the High Hill



**Lianchhiari**, the legendary 19th-century poetess from the village of Dungtlang, is often called the "Sappho of the Hills." Her verses are the pinnacle of romantic Mizo poetry.

## The Theme of Longing
Most of Lianchhiari's work revolves around her unrequited love for **Chawngfianga**. Her poetry is unique for its vivid use of topographical metaphors. She often compared the distance between lovers to the deep valleys and mist-covered ridges of the **[[Champhai_District]]**.

## The "Lianchhiari Lung" (Lianchhiari's Rock)
She is said to have composed her greatest works while sitting on a massive flat rock overlooking the Tiau River valley. This site, **Lianchhiari Lung**, is now a heritage monument. Her verses often describe the wind (*Thli*) and the wandering of the soul (*Thlarau*) across the ridges.

## Translation Challenges
Translating her work into English is notoriously difficult due to the "high Mizo" (archaic) vocabulary she used. Modern scholars like **[[Lalthangfala_Khiangte]]** have attempted to capture her lyrical meter, which relies on a specific vowel-consonant harmony characteristic of the **[[Mizo_Alphabet]]**.
