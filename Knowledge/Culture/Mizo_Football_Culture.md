# Mizo Football Culture



Football is not merely a sport in Mizoram — it is deeply embedded in the social and emotional fabric of Mizo society.

## History
Football was introduced by British administrators and missionaries in the early 20th century. The game spread rapidly through the church youth network, displacing traditional sports (*Inbuan* wrestling, archery). By the mid-20th century, nearly every village had a football pitch.

## Modern Excellence
Today Mizoram produces an extraordinarily high per-capita concentration of quality Indian footballers. The state has:
- **Aizawl FC**: First-ever Northeast Indian club to win the I-League (2016–17 season) against all odds on a shoestring budget — an internationally celebrated underdog story.
- **[[Jeje_Lalpekhlua]]**: India's greatest-ever Mizo footballer.
- The **Mizoram Premier League (MPL)**: A fiercely competitive state football league.
