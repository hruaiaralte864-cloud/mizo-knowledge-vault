# Traditional Mizo Musical Instruments

**Tags:** #culture #music #art

Mizo music was traditionally rhythmic and vocal-focused, utilizing instruments made primarily from forest materials like bamboo, brass, and animal skins.

## Key Instruments
- **Khuang:** The quintessential Mizo drum, often made from hollowed trunks with goat skin heads. It is the heart of [[Hlado_And_Music|Hlado]] and church singing.
- **Dar:** Large brass gongs, often of Burmese origin, prized as family wealth and used in ceremonial war dances.
- **Tumphit:** A set of small bamboo pipes tied together, producing a shrill, haunting whistle used to signal in the jungle.
- **Bengbung:** A simple bamboo Jews-harp, often used as a quiet, personal instrument during courtship.
