# Puanchei



**Puanchei** is the most colorful and elaborate of all Mizo traditional textiles. It is the signature garment worn by Mizo women during festivals and significant ceremonies.

## Design and Weaving
Puanchei is characterized by its bold horizontal stripes of red, black, and white, intricately woven with supplementary weft patterns. The weaving process is extremely time-consuming, requiring exceptional skill on the loin loom.

## Symbolism
Unlike the [[Tawlhloh_Puan_Legend|Tawlhloh Puan]] which was for warriors, the Puanchei is a celebration of womanhood and Mizo craftsmanship. It is the primary dress for the [[Cheraw_Dance]] and is a must-have item in a Mizo woman's wardrobe.
