﻿# Musical Node: Dar-khuang Sacred Gong



## 1. Technical Classification
- **Category**: Percussion
- **Primary Material**: Brass/Bronze
- **Primary Function**: Traditional Orchestration and Storytelling.

## 2. Acoustic Design
The Dar-khuang Sacred Gong utilizes the resonance properties of local **[[Bamboo_Ecology|Bamboo]]** or mountain hardwoods. Its frequency is designed to carry across deep valleys, allowing for communication between ridge-top settlements.

## 3. Cultural Value
The most valued ensemble of gongs, used for high-status rituals. It is central to the **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition]]** and is the heartbeat of festivals like **[[Chapchar_Kut_Festival]]**.

## 4. Lyrical Structure
In Mizo music theory, the **[[Hla-phuah-thiam]]** (composer) often uses the Percussion as a rhythmic spine for the **[[Linguistic_Analysis_of_Mizo_Tones|Tonal Melidies]]** of the lyrics.
