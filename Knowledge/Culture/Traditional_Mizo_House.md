# Traditional Mizo Architecture

**Tags:** #culture #architecture #history

Building a Mizo house was a communal effort involving the entire village, a prime example of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**.

## Structure
Houses were built on wooden or bamboo stilts to handle the steep terrain and the heavy monsoon rains.
- **Leisat:** The dirt-floor area under the house used for storing firewood and keeping livestock.
- **Bathlar:** The open wooden terrace at the entrance.
- **Layout:** A single large room divided by mats, with a central hearth (**[[Mizo_Cuisine|Tap-pui]]**) that served as the heart of the home.
