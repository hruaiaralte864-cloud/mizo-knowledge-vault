# Puanpui (The Great Quilt)



**Puanpui** is a thick, white, hand-woven cotton blanket. It is unique among Mizo textiles because it is not worn as clothing but used as a prestigious bedding item.

## Craftsmanship
Making a Puanpui is a labor of love. It involves a "shaggy" weaving technique where thousands of small cotton tufts are woven into the base fabric to create a heavy, warm, and highly durable quilt.

## Symbolic Value
A Puanpui was traditionally a part of a bride's dowry. It was also given as a tribute to heroes. Because of its weight and the effort required to make it, a Puanpui is considered a family heirloom of the highest order.
