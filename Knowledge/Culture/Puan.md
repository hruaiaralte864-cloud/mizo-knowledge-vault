# Puan — Traditional Mizo Textiles

**Tags:** #culture #art #craft

**Puan** is the handwoven textile garment that forms the cornerstone of Mizo traditional dress and a major artistic tradition.

## Notable Varieties
- **Puanchei:** The most glamorous puan worn at festivals and weddings. Bright red and black stripes with intricate geometric patterns, embellished with golden thread.
- **Kawrchei:** A heavy, thick puan traditionally associated with the Thangchhuah achievement; features deep blue and black interlocked patterns.
- **Ngotekherh:** Worn by women as a daily garment; simpler geometric stripes in muted colours.

Mizo weaving is done on traditional backstrap or foot-treadle looms. Communities like the **Mara, Lai, and Pawi** people in south Mizoram have particularly distinct and elaborate textile traditions.
