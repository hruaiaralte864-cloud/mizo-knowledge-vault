# Mizo Choir Uniforms: A Fabric of Identity



The **Mizo Choir** is a global cultural ambassador, and the uniforms worn by these choirs are sophisticated symbols of Mizo identity, blending traditional textiles with modern choral aesthetics.

## The Puan in Uniforms
The core element of any Mizo choir uniform is the **[[Mizo_Handicrafts_Textiles]]**, specifically the *Puan*. Choirs often use the **Puanchei** (the most colorful and ornate Puan) or custom-designed patterns that reflect their specific church or organization.
- **Men's Attire:** Typically involves a waistcoat (Puan hlan) or a sash made from handloom fabric.
- **Women's Attire:** A full-length Puan-skirt, often color-coordinated with the choir's theme.

## Symbolic Colors
- **Red and Black**: Traditional colors symbolizing bravery and history.
- **White**: Often used in church choirs to symbolize purity and peace (**[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**).

## Global Stage
When Mizo choirs travel to competitions like the World Choir Games, their uniforms are a primary way they showcase the "Mizo Wave." The intricate weaving patterns are often the first thing that catches the audience's eye, leading to a surge in interest for **[[Mizoram_Tourism_Development_Authority]]** initiatives.
