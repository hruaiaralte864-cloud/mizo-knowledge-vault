# Architecture Node: Tap-chhak Hearth Design

**Tags:** #engineering #architecture #construction #Mizoram #research #IndigenousTech

## 1. Technical Classification
- **Category**: Domestic
- **Primary Material**: Clay, stone, hanging bamboo rack
- **Structural Philosophy**: Resilience in High-Altitude Sloped Terrain.

## 2. Engineering Principles
The Tap-chhak Hearth Design utilizes a specialized post-and-lintel system optimized for the seismic zones of the **[[Indo-Myanmar_Frontier|Mizo Hills]]**. The use of **[[Bamboo_Ecology|Bamboo]]** lashings instead of rigid nails allows the structure to flex during tremors without collapsing.

## 3. Functional Design
The technical center of the house, providing heat and food preservation smoke. This was a critical component of the **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition]]**, ensuring survival against the elements and rival clans.

## 4. Resource Management
Materials were sourced sustainably from the **[[Jhum_Cultivation_Cycle]]** buffer zones, ensuring that the **[[Mizoram_Subtropical_Pine_Forest]]** remained balanced despite the needs of a growing settlement.
