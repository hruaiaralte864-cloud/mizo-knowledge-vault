# Mizo Death Rites (Thane)

**Tags:** #culture #ritual #religion

The Mizo approach to death is characterized by deep communal support, managed largely by the **[[Young_Mizo_Association|YMA]]** in modern times.

## Thane (Mourning)
When a person dies, the entire neighbourhood stops work. Young men from the YMA gather to dig the grave, while women prepare food for the grieving family. This period of intense communal mourning and support is called **Thane**.

## Memory Stones
Historically, for a warrior or a great chief, a **Lungpui** (memorial stone) would be erected. Today, these have been replaced by elaborate modern tombstones, but the custom of maintaining the grave and visiting it as a family remains very strong.
