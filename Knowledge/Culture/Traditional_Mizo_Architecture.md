# Traditional Mizo House Architecture

**Tags:** #culture #architecture #society

The traditional Mizo longhouse (*In*) was engineered brilliantly for its mountainous, seismically active environment.

## Construction
- **Materials:** Split-bamboo walls, bamboo-strip flooring raised on stilts, and woven bamboo-mat roofing topped with thick layers of thatch grass.
- **Structure:** Long, rectangular floor plan running along the ridge axis of the hill. The stilt construction kept the floor dry and protected from serpents and insects.
- **The Central Hearth (*Mitthi Khua*):** Every house had a central open fireplace over which a bamboo rack (*Chhang*) was suspended — used to smoke preserve meat (*[[Mizo_Cuisine|Vawksa Rep]]*), dry firewood, and store grain.
- **The *Zanpui* (headroom):** A large raised storage loft above the hearth where the family kept its most valuable possessions.

## Defensive Design
Villages were always built on ridges. Home entries were through low doorways requiring any attacker to stoop and expose their back — a deliberate defensive measure.
