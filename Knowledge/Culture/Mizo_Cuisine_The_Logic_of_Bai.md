# Mizo Cuisine: The Logic of "Bai"

**Tags:** #culture #cuisine #health

Mizo cuisine is defined by **Bai**, a vegetable stew that is the center of every meal. Its "logic" is efficiency, health, and flavor extraction from the jungle.

## The "Bai" Formula
A standard *Bai* requires three elements:
1. **The Base**: Water and **[[Mizo_Fermentation_Science_Bekang_Saum]]** (Sa-um or Bekang).
2. **The Catalyst**: *Chingal* (ash-water) or Soda to soften the greens.
3. **The Forage**: A mix of seasonal greens (mustard, bamboo shoots, or bitter eggplant).
The result is a nutrient-dense, low-oil stew that provides the high fiber needed for active mountain living.

## Meat Smoking and Preservation
Before modern refrigeration, Mizos preserved meat by smoking it over the kitchen hearth (*Tapchaw*). This "Deep Smoke" flavor is a hallmark of the **[[Mizo_Culinary_Arts]]**. Smoked pork and beef are the primary proteins in the **[[Mizo_Marriage_Gifts_Thul]]** feasts.

## The "Zu" (Rice Beer) History
Historically, *Zu* was the primary social lubricant and ritual offering. It was brewed from rice and fermented with a local yeast starter. Since the **[[Mizoram_Liquor_Prohibition_Act]]**, the public consumption of *Zu* has shifted to "Grape Wine" and other state-regulated alternatives, though the "Zu Culture" remains a major theme in **[[Mizo_Poetry_Eras]]**.
