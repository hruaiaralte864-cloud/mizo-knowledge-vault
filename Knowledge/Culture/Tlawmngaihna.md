# Tlawmngaihna — The Mizo Ethical Code



**Tlawmngaihna** (*tlawm* = modest/humble, *ngaihna* = esteem/regard) is the foundational philosophy of Mizo civilisation. It is widely considered untranslatable by a single English word, but its essence is:

> *The spirit of selfless sacrifice, chivalry, and going beyond the call of duty for others without expectation of reward.*

## In Practice
- When someone dies, the entire village community drops all work immediately and assists the bereaved family — building the coffin, digging the grave, cooking food. This practice remains sacred today.
- A man must give the most dangerous or tiring task to himself.
- A host must give the best portion of food and the best sleeping place to the guest.

## Modern Legacy
The **[[Tlawmngaihna_Award]]** is awarded by the [[Young_Mizo_Association]] for acts of extraordinary selfless heroism. It is universally considered the highest honour in Mizo civil society.
