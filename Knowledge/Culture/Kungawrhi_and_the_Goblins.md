# Mythology Node: Kungawrhi and the Goblins

**Tags:** #culture #mythology #folklore #legends #Mizoram #research #Spirituality

## 1. Narrative Classification
- **Category**: Myth
- **Key Entity**: Kungawrhi, Keichala
- **Narrative Focus**: Ancestral Memory and Spiritual Worldview.

## 2. Mythic Context
The Kungawrhi and the Goblins is a foundational narrative in **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition]]**. These stories were traditionally passed down through oral storytelling in the **[[Zawlbuk_Youth_Dormitory|Zawlbuk]]**, serving as a moral and spiritual compass for the community.

## 3. Cultural Symbology
The story of a beautiful girl abducted by tree spirits and her rescue. This myth highlights the Mizo relationship with the **[[Mizoram_Subtropical_Pine_Forest|Natural World]]** and the belief in a thinning veil between the living and the spirits of the **[[Mithikhuah_City_of_Dead|Underworld]]**.

## 4. Modern Interpretation
Modern Mizo writers and artists in **[[Aizawl_City_Data]]** are now re-interpreting these legends in literature and animation, ensuring that the **[[Myth]]** legacy informs the digital future of the Zo people.
