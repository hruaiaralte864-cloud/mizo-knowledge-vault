# The Zawlbuk — Bachelors' Dormitory



The **Zawlbuk** was the communal dormitory for all unmarried men (*Tlangval*) of a Mizo village. It was positioned right next to the [[Lal_System|Chief's house]] and served as the military, educational, and social heart of traditional village life.

## Roles
- **Defence:** All able-bodied men slept here, enabling an instant rapid-response militia.
- **Education:** Boys (*Hmeichhe thalai*) were rigorously trained in oral history, combat, and ethical values by their elders.
- **Hospitality:** All male visitors to the village were hosted in the Zawlbuk.

## Strict Hierarchy
The dormitory operated on a tyrannical seniority ladder:
- **Thingnawi:** Young boys tasked with collecting firewood for the central hearth (*Maimawm*).
- **Tlangval:** Young unmarried adult men.
- **Val Upa:** The respected elder leader of the dormitory.

## Decline
With the arrival of [[Missionary_Arrival|Christian missionaries]] and formal schooling, the Zawlbuk became obsolete by the mid-20th century. Its social-disciplinary role was effectively inherited by the **[[Young_Mizo_Association]]**.
