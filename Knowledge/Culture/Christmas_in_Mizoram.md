# Christmas in Mizoram

**Tags:** #culture #religion #festivals

Christmas is the most significant and widely celebrated festival in Mizoram. For the Mizo people, it is not just a religious event but the ultimate social and cultural homecoming.

## Historical Context
The first Christmas in Mizoram was celebrated in **1893** by the British troops at Aizawl, but it only became a Mizo tradition after the arrival of missionaries and the mass conversion to Christianity in the early 20th century.

## Unique Traditions
### 1. Urhsunna (Holy Preparation)
The preparation for Christmas often starts in November. Every household and every street is decorated with stars, lights, and streamers.

### 2. Christmas Feast (Ruaithe)
The **Ruaithe** is a grand communal feast organized by the church. Every member of the community, regardless of status, sits together to eat. Large herds of cattle and pigs are slaughtered for this feast.

### 3. Zaipawl (Choirs)
Mizos are world-renowned for their choral singing. During Christmas, every local church choir practices for months to perform classic Händel's *Messiah* pieces and original Mizo hymns.

### 4. Street Carols
In many villages, groups of young people still go door-to-door singing carols, often accompanied by the traditional **[[Khuang]]** (drum).

## The Spirit of Christmas
Christmas in Mizoram is defined by **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**. It is a time when the community ensures that no family is without food or joy. Many people return from all over the world to their home villages just for these three days (Dec 24-26).
