# Evolution of Mizo Marriage and the "Man" (Bride Price)



Mizo marriage is a complex synthesis of ancient clan traditions and modern Christian liturgy. The central element remains the **Man** (Bride Price), which has survived the transition into modernity.

## The Ancient Root: The "Man"
Traditionally, the "Man" was paid in Mithun (Bison), gongs, or beads. It was not a "sale" of the woman, but a "Security Deposit" to the bride's clan. If the marriage succeeded, the wealth stayed; if it failed due to the husband's fault, the "Man" was forfeited. This was the foundation of the **[[Traditional_Justice_System]]** regarding family life.

## The Modern Synthesis
Today, the "Man" is paid in a symbolic cash amount (e.g., Rs. 420 or Rs. 1000) inside the **[[Mizo_Marriage_Gifts_Thul]]**. The ceremony usually takes place in a church but is preceded by the "Man-hlan" (giving of the price) at the bride's home. The **[[Young_Mizo_Association_YMA]]** plays a critical role in managing the wedding logistics, ensuring that no family is overburdened by costs.

## Legal Protection
The **[[Mizo_Customary_Law_Registry]]** governs these transactions. Under **[[Article_371G_Analysis]]**, these marriage rites are protected from external interference, ensuring that the communal and clan-based logic of Mizo family life remains intact.
