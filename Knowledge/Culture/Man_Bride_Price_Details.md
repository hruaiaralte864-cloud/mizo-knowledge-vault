# 'Man' (The Mizo Bride Price)



In Mizo culture, marriage involves the payment of a bride price called **Man**. This is not seen as a "purchase" but as a gesture of respect and a bond between households.

## Components of the Price
The total price is often split into specific named portions:
- **Tluangman:** The main portion of the price given to the bride's father.
- **Sumṭan:** A portion given to the bride's paternal aunt (*Ni-pui*).
- **Lawmchal:** A small payment to the bride's closest friend or "best man."
- **Zophei:** A portion historically given to the bride's eldest brother.

## Ritual Value
The negotiation of the 'Man' is a formal social event involving elders from both sides. It ensures that the marriage is recognized and supported by the entire clan. Today, while the amounts are often symbolic, the ritual remains a cornerstone of Mizo marriage.
