# Mizo Etymology: The Myth of 'Lunglei'

**Tags:** #linguistics #history #mythology

The naming of **[[Lunglei_District]]** is a classic example of Mizo toponymy (place-naming) being intertwined with geological and mythological features.

## Linguistic Origin
The word "Lunglei" is derived from two Mizo words:
1. **Lung**: Stone or Rock.
2. **Le**: Bridge.

Literal Meaning: **"The Stone Bridge"**.

## The Mythological Bridge
According to local lore, there was a natural rock formation resembling a bridge over the Nghasih stream near the town. This "bridge" was believed to have been built by spirits or giants in the ancient past. The term "Lunglei" became synonymous with the entire settlement that grew around this landmark.

## Significance in Identity
The myth reinforces the Mizo deep connection with their mountainous landscape. It serves as a linguistic marker for the **[[Lunglei_District]]**, distinguishing its identity from the northern Aizawl-centric culture.
