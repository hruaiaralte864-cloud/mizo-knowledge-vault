# Hmar Language

**Tags:** #linguistics #culture #identity

The **Hmar language** is a major member of the Kukish branch of the Bodic group of the Tibeto-Burman language family. It is spoken by the Hmar people across Northeast India, particularly in Mizoram, Manipur, and Assam.

## Classification
Hmar is closely related to the Lusei (Duhlian) dialect of Mizo. In fact, many linguistic scholars consider them part of the same language continuum.

## Features
1. **Tonality**: Like most Tibeto-Burman languages, Hmar is a tonal language where the pitch of a word can change its meaning.
2. **Alphabet**: Hmar uses the Roman script, which was introduced by Christian missionaries in the late 19th century.
3. **Mutual Intelligibility**: There is a high degree of mutual intelligibility between Hmar and the standard Mizo language.

## Significance
Hmar has a rich oral tradition of folk songs (*Hla*) and tales (*Thawnthu*). The preservation of the Hmar dialect is a key priority for the Hmar Literature Society and the **[[Mizo_Academy_of_Letters_MAL]]**.
