# Mizoram State Archives: The Indo-Burma Files



The **Mizoram State Archives** in Aizawl holds one of the most important collections of colonial and post-colonial records in Northeast India, specifically the "Indo-Burma Files."

## Historical Record Keeping
The Indo-Burma files contain rare correspondence between the British Frontier Officers and the various **[[Sailo_Chiefs]]**. These records provide the primary source material for understanding the **[[Lushai_Expeditions]]** and the subsequent administrative division of the Mizo hills.

## Research Value
Historians like **[[B_Lalthangliana_Ancient_History]]** have extensively used these archives to reconstruct the migration patterns and border treaties that define modern Mizoram. The archives also house the "Bamboo Famine" records, documenting the state's response to the periodic **[[Mautam_Famine_Cycle]]**.

## Digitization Efforts
As part of the modern **[[Governance_Overview]]** initiatives, the archives are undergoing a massive digitization project to make these rare manuscripts accessible to global researchers while preserving the fragile physical copies.
