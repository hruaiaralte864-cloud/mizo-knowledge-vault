# Geography of the Mizo Hills

Mizoram, known as the "Land of the Hill People," is a land of dramatic verticality. Its geography is characterized by alternating steep ridges and narrow valleys, creating a "corrugated" landscape.

## 🏔️ The Ridge-and-Valley System
The hills in Mizoram generally run in a **North-South direction**. 
- **The Fold Mountains**: The Mizo Hills are young fold mountains, part of the Rakhine Yoma (Arakan Yoma) range and the Tripura-Mizoram fold belt.
- **Orientation**: The western ridges are generally lower, while the height increases toward the east.

## 🌊 Major Rivers & Hydrology
Mizoram is a land of high rainfall (over 2500mm annually) but seasonal water scarcity due to the steep terrain.
- **Tlawng River**: The longest river in Mizoram, flowing northward into the Barak valley.
- **Chhimtuipui (Kolodyne)**: The largest river in the south, vital for the Kaladan Multi-modal Transit Project connecting India to Myanmar.
- **Tuirial & Tuivawl**: Significant rivers for power generation and agriculture.

## ⛰️ Key Summits
1. **Phawngpui (Blue Mountain)**: The highest peak at 2,157 meters, located near the Myanmar border. It is a sacred site and a national park.
2. **Reiek & Hmuifang**: Famous scenic cliffs that offer panoramic views of the Bangladesh plains in the west.

## 🌦️ Climate Zones
- **Humid Subtropical**: Most of the state enjoys a temperate climate.
- **Summer (Rainy)**: May to September, dominated by the Southwest Monsoon.
- **Winter**: November to February, marked by deep morning mists (*Lunglenna* mists) in the valleys.
