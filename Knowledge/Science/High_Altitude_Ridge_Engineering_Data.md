# High-Altitude Engineering: Ridge Stability and Soil Mechanics

, focusing on **Soil Shear Strength** and **Slenderness Ratios** in mountain construction.

## 1. Soil Composition and Mechanical Data
The geology of the Lushai Hills primarily consists of **Surma Group** tertiary rocks (shale, siltstone, and sandstone).

| Parameter | Data Range (Aizawl District) | Engineering Impact |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Soil Texture** | Sandy Silt to Silty Clay | High permeability, low cohesion when wet. |
| **Shear Strength** | 20 kPa to 45 kPa | Requires specialized "Step-Foundation" piling. |
| **Angle of Repose** | 25° to 35° | Exceeded by 60% of existing urban structures. |
| **Pore Water Pressure** | High (during Monsoon) | Primary trigger for "Rotational Landslides." |

## 2. Ridge Engineering Logic: The "Step-Foundation"
Conventional horizontal foundations are prone to failure on the 30°+ slopes of **[[Aizawl_District]]**.
- **The "Step" Design**: Modern research at **[[NIT_Mizoram_Overview]]** promotes the use of staggered foundations that distribute the weight across multiple layers of rock rather than a single footprint.
- **Retaining Wall Bio-Engineering**: Utilizing the root systems of native grasses (see **[[Broom_Grass_Ecology]]**) to naturally stabilize topsoil layers above concrete retaining structures.

## 3. High-Altitude Structural Dynamics
Building "Vertical" on a "Vertical" ridge creates unique wind-shear and seismic challenges.
- **Slenderness Ratio**: Research suggests a maximum height-to-width ratio of **3:1** for structures on the middle ridges to prevent "Base Shear" failure during Category V earthquakes.
- **Seismic Resonance**: Studying how the natural frequency of the ridges in **[[Champhai_District]]** interacts with the vibration of high-density housing during tremors.

## 4. Hydraulic Engineering in the Hills
- **Vertical Water Lift**: Data from the **Greater Aizawl Water Supply Scheme (Phase II)** shows that moving water from the Tlawng River (350m) to the Aizawl Peak (1,150m) requires a massive 800m vertical head, creating one of the most energy-intensive municipal water systems in India.
