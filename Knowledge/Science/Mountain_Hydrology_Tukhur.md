# Mountain Hydrology: The Tukhur System

, Mizo villages—perched on high ridges—relied on a sophisticated system of mountain hydrology centered around the **Tukhur** (Water Spring).

## The Engineering of the Spring
A Tukhur is not just a hole in the ground; it is an engineered water-collection point. Traditionally, villagers would use hollowed-out bamboo pipes (*Vau*) to channel water from deep crevices in the rock-face into a central stone-lined basin. This ensured a filtered, continuous supply of water even during the dry season.

## Communal Management
Maintenance of the Tukhur was a primary **[[Hnatlang_as_Development_Tool]]** activity. Every month, the youth would clear the debris and ensure the bamboo conduits were clear. The cleanliness of the village spring was a matter of extreme pride and was protected by strict **[[Mizo_Customary_Law_Registry]]** rules.

## Evolution to Modern Water Policy
Under the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]**, the state is Revitalizing these traditional springs to augment modern water supplies. The **[[Mizoram_State_Planning_Board]]** recognizes that these ancient "Water Chambers" are key to rural sustainability, especially in the water-scarce **[[Serchhip_District]]**.
