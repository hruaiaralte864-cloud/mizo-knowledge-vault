# Mizo Blacksmithing: The Science of Thirkhuahtu

**Tags:** #science #metallurgy #manual_science #thir

The **Thirkhuahtu** (Blacksmith) was a critical figure in ancient Mizo villages, responsible for the tools of both survival and war.

## Blacksmithing Tempering
Mizo blacksmiths used a sophisticated "Water Quenching" and "Air Cooling" cycle to temper their iron (*Thir*). 
- **The Forge**: Fueled by high-density charcoal from specific hill woods.
- **Metallurgical Logic**: The goal was always a "Work-Hardened Edge." By hammer-forging local iron into the **[[Mizo_Weapons_Science_Chempui_Crossbow]]**, they created blades that were tough enough to bridge the gap between hard bamboo and soft wood.

## The Village Bellows
A unique design involving two vertical bamboo cylinders and a feathered piston, providing a constant high-velocity oxygen stream to the forge—a testament to early Mizo fluid mechanics.
