# Mizo Astronomy: Arsi-te (The Stars)

, using the movement of stars, or **Arsi-te**, to regulate their agrarian lifestyle and navigate the dense mountain jungles.

## Key Constellations and Stars
- **Zatla-ersi**: Traditionally interpreted as the "Morning Star," its rising indicated the time to prepare for the day's **[[Jhum_Cultivation]]**.
- **Chhohraw-arsi**: Associated with the legend of two hunters, this group of stars was used to mark the transition from the rainy season to the harvest season.
- **Sikni-arsi**: The appearance of this cluster in the winter sky signaled the beginning of the **[[Chapchar_Kut]]** preparations.

## Celestial Navigation
Mizo hunters and warriors relied on the orientation of the "Milky Way" (known as *Zaim-thur*) to find their way home across ridge systems like those in the **[[Mamit_District]]**. The precision of these observations was crucial for survival in an era before mapped **[[Geography_Overview]]**.

## Mythology and Science
For the ancient Mizos, the stars were not just physical bodies but celestial markers of the great heroes of **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]**. This fusion of myth and science provided a holistic view of the universe that is now a major research interest for the **[[Mizoram_University_MZU]]** department of history and ethnography.
