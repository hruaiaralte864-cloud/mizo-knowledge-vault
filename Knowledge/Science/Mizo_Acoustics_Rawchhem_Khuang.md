# Mizo Acoustics: The Science of Bamboo Sound

"Material Science"—the specific resonant frequencies of the hill bamboo and animal hides.

## The Rawchhem (Bamboo Bagpipes)
The **Rawchhem** is a unique Mizo polyphonic instrument. It consists of multiple bamboo pipes inserted into a dried gourd. Each pipe contains a small reed. The acoustic logic involves maintaining a "Drone" while playing a melody, a sophisticated use of wind-pressure mechanics that mimics the sound of mountain cicadas.

## The Khuang (Mizo Drum)
The **[[Khuang_The_Mizo_Drum]]** is the heart of Mizo choral music.
- **The Body**: Carved from the center of **[[Areca_Nut_Soil_Impact]]** or Jackfruit trees for low-frequency resonance.
- **The Hide**: Goat or cow hide is stretched and tuned using heat and moisture.
The science of "Khuang Beating" involves precise coordination with the **[[Mizo_Choral_Science_and_Solfa]]** to maintain the communal heartbeat.

## The Tumphit
A series of hollow bamboo pipes of varying lengths, the **Tumphit** creates specific harmonics. Its acoustics are based on the "Closed-Closed" or "Open-Closed" pipe physics, producing the distinct haunting melodies of the Lusei hills.
