# The Physics of Insuknawr (Rod-Pushing)

**Tags:** #science #physics #sports #culture

**Insuknawr** is the national game of Mizoram, a unique test of strength that originated from the physical labor of rice husking.

## The Mechanics of the "Suk"
The only instrument used is the **Suk**—a heavy wooden pestle. 
- **Leverage**: Two players stand inside a 4-meter circle, tucking the Suk under their armpits. The game is a study in "Torque and Counter-Torque."
- **Biomechanics**: Unlike tug-of-war, which is a pulling force, Insuknawr is a pure "Compression" force. Players must lower their center of gravity and use their calf and thigh muscles to generate forward thrust while maintaining balance on the "Pivot Point" (the circle center).

## Other "Suk" Variants
- **Sukkhawh**: Throwing the Suk like a javelin (Ballistics).
- **Insukherh**: A twisting duel using the Suk to test rotational wrist strength.
