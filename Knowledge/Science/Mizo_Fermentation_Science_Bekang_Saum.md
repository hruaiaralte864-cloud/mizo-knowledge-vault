# Mizo Fermentation Science: Sa-um and Bekang

"The Chemistry of Decay"—the controlled fermentation of proteins and fats to create intense umami flavors.

## Sa-um: The Fermented Pig Fat
*Sa-um* is the quintessential Mizo flavoring agent. It is prepared by cleaning pig fat, boiling it, and then storing it in a sealed gourd or container for several weeks. The resulting "fermented lard" is used in almost every **[[Mizo_Cuisine_The_Logic_of_Bai]]**. Microbiologically, it is a stable fat-fermentation that provides a unique pungency comparable to blue cheese or truffles.

## Bekang-um: The Soybean Culture
*Bekang* is fermented soybean, similar to Japanese *Natto* or Manipuri *Hawaijar*. It is rich in *Bacillus subtilis*. The beans are boiled, wrapped in large leaves (*Hnahthial*), and kept in a warm place (often near the hearth) for 2-3 days. This process breaks down complex proteins into easily digestible amino acids, a vital source of protein in the historical mountain diet.

## Chingal: The Alkaline Catalyst
Mizo cooking often uses *Chingal*—a liquid obtained by filtering water through wood ash. This traditional alkali acts as a catalyst in the **[[Mizo_Cuisine_The_Logic_of_Bai]]**, softening tough fibers and enhancing the extraction of nutrients. This is a primary subject of study at **[[Mizoram_University_MZU]]**’s food science department.
