# Mizo Ethnobotany: Traditional Healing Plants

**[[Mamit_District]]** and **[[Lunglei_District]]** are a living pharmacy of **Mizo Ethnobotany**, containing hundreds of plants used for generations to treat everything from fever to snakebites.

## Primary Medicinal Flora
- **Hnim-rhing**: A versatile plant used for its antibiotic properties to treat skin infections and wounds.
- **Thing-thei**: The bark of certain wild fig trees is processed into a tonic for digestive ailments.
- **Ram-hmachal**: Used in the treatment of malaria—a historical scourge of the **[[Lushai_Hills]]**.

## The Healing Philosophy
Mizo traditional medicine, or "Hnim-hna hman," is holistic. It often combines the physical application of the plant with the **[[Steam_Bath_Traditional_Healing]]** technique to "open the pores" and release toxins. This practice is rooted in the belief that the forest provides a cure for every mountain ailment.

## Modern Research and IP
The **[[Mizoram_University_MZU]]** and the **[[Mizoram_State_Pollution_Control_Board]]** are currently documenting these plants to protect them from "Bio-piracy." Several traditional remedies are being tested for modern pharmacological applications, potentially creating a new export wing for the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]**.
