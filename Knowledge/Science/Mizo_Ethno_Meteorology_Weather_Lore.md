# Mizo Ethno-Meteorology: The Science of Clouds and Birds

-science #farming

Before the advent of modern satellite forecasting, Mizo farmers relied on a sophisticated "Biological Radar" to predict the heavy Mizo monsoon.

## The Hornbill Indicator (Vakhu)
The behavior of the **Great Pied Hornbill** (*Vakhu*) is a primary indicator. When these birds fly low and change their nesting calls, it signaled the onset of the "Little Monsoon" (*Ruahphunani*). This bio-indicator is still cross-referenced by local farmers against the **[[Mizoram_Remote_Sensing_Centre]]** data.

## The Cicada Calendar
The frequency and timing of cicada bursts (*Sialki*) inform the "Traditional Planting Windows." Mizo elders mapped different species of cicadas to specific humidity levels, creating a high-resolution "Sound-Clock" for the **[[Jhum_Cultivation_Sustainability]]** cycles.

## Cloud Nomenclature (Chhum)
Mizo language has precise terms for mountain clouds:
- **Chhum-dum**: Heavy rain clouds.
- **Chhum-vawh**: The morning-mist that protects tea crops.
- **Chhum-tla**: Low-hanging clouds used for ambush cover in **[[Ridge_Fortress_Engineering_Mizo]]**.
