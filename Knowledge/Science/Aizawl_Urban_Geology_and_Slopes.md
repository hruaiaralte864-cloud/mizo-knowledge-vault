# Aizawl Urban Geology and Slope Stability

**Tags:** #science #geology #urban_planning #disaster

Aizawl is built on a series of North-South trending ridges composed of splintery shale and compact sandstone. This unique **[[Geography_Overview]]** makes urban geology a matter of survival for the city.

## Structural Vulnerability
The shale layers in Aizawl are prone to "sliding" when saturated with monsoon rains. This led to historic events like the **[[The_Haupu_Landslide_Disaster]]**. Most of Aizawl's residential areas are built on slopes exceeding 30 degrees, which is technically beyond the safe limit for conventional construction.

## Engineering Solutions: "Vertical Stabilization"
Under the **[[Mizoram_State_Planning_Board]]**, new building bylaws mandate "Slope Stability Analysis" before any multi-story construction. Techniques include:
- **Retaining Walls (Gabions)**: Using wire-mesh baskets filled with stones to allow for drainage while holding the slope.
- **Deep Piling**: Driving concrete pillars into the hard sandstone layers beneath the shale.
- **Surface Drainage Management**: Ensuring that rainwater is channeled away from the "Slip-planes" to prevent soil lubrication.

## The Future: Landslide Early Warning
The **[[Mizoram_University_MZU]]** is collaborating with the Geological Survey of India to deploy real-time sensors that measure "Soil Moisture Intensity." These are linked to the **[[Mizoram_Cyber_Security_Initiative]]** and can broadcast evacuation alerts via the **[[Aizawl_Post_Daily]]** and other media during high-risk rain events.
