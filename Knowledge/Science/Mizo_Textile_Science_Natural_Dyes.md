# Mizo Textile Science: Patterns and Dyes

**Tags:** #science #textiles #culture #natural_dyes

Mizo handloom is not just an art form; it is a structural science involving the complex intersection of plant chemistry and loom mathematics.

## The Chemistry of "Ting" (Indigo)
Traditionally, the "Mizo Black" and "Mizo Blue" found in the **[[Puandum]]** and **[[Puanchei]]** were obtained from the **Ting** (Indigofera) plant. The leaves were fermented in water with alkaline ash (*Chingal*) to reduce the indigo into a soluble form, which then oxidized into a permanent blue upon exposure to air—a classic "Vat Dyeing" process.

## Weaving Mathematics
The **Loin Loom** (*Puan bu*) used by Mizo women requires precise tension calculations. Patterns like the "Row of Diamonds" or "Zig-Zags" are achieved through "String Heddle" logic—a binary-like system where specific warp threads are lifted to create geometric structures. This is a primary focus of the **[[Startup_Ecosystem_Aizawl]]** fashion-tech startups.

## Functional Significance
Patterns are not merely decorative; they act as "Clan Signifiers." The **[[Tawlhloh_Puan_Legend]]**, for instance, was traditionally a "Warrior's Cloth," with its technical complexity serving as a testament to the weaver's skill and the wearer's status.
