# Mizo Ethno-Astronomy: Constellations and Navigation

**Tags:** #science #astronomy #navigation #culture #folklore #research

This node archives the traditional Mizo knowledge of the celestial sphere, documenting how ancient tribes utilized the stars for time-keeping, agriculture, and mountain navigation.

## 1. Mizo Constellations (Arsi Rual)
The Mizo identified specific star clusters to represent terrestrial objects and myths.

| Mizo Name | Western Equivalent (Approx) | Mythological/Practical Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Siruk** | Pleiades (Seven Sisters) | "The Six" - Used to determine the start of the **[[Jhum_Cultivation_Cycle]]**. |
| **Zangkhua** | Ursa Major (Big Dipper) | Representing the "backbone" of the sky. |
| **Khiangte Arsi** | Orion's Belt | Associated with the **[[Khiangte_Clan]]** and protective hunters. |
| **Kong-ko-re** | Venus (Evening Star) | The "Path-Lighter" for evening social visits. |
| **Sawi-nga** | Cassiopeia (W-shape) | Representing five bent nodes of bamboo. |

## 2. The Agricultural Celestial Clock
Star positions determined the communal labor schedule mandated by the **[[Village_Council_Structure]]**.
- **The Descent of Siruk**: When the Pleiades touched the western horizon at dusk, it signaled the deadline for finishing the cutting of jhum forests.
- **The Rise of the Red Star (Arsi Sen)**: Associated with the onset of the heavy moonsoon rains and the time to plant **[[Mizo_Ginger_Sawhthing]]**.

## 3. Lunar Eclipse Logic: "Chawngmawii and the Sun"
Mizo folklore provides a unique "Proto-Scientific" explanation for eclipses:
- **Chawngmawii and Hrangchhuana**: The moon and sun are lovers who meet once a year. When they embrace, their light is dimmed, creating an eclipse.
- **Ritual Response**: Villages traditionally beat drums to "wake" the sun, ensuring it wasn't swallowed by the celestial dragon (**[[Zite_Dragon]]**).

## 4. Navigation by Ridgeline Stars
Before the compass, Mizo travelers used the "Star-to-Peak" alignment. They would memorize which stars "rested" on specific peaks (like **[[Phawngpui_Peak]]**) at certain months to navigate through the dense forest at night.
