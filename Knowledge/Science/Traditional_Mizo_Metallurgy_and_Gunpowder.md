# Traditional Mizo Metallurgy and Gunpowder Chemistry

.

## 1. The "Kawlbawm" (Mizo Flintlock) Engineering
The Mizo flintlock rifle, though inspired by British designs, was locally manufactured with specific modifications for mountain terrain.

| Component | Material | Engineering Feature |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Barrel** | Iron/Brass Mix | "Thick-walled" to prevent bursting in high humidity. |
| **The Cock** | Forged Steel | Tuned for heavy impact to ensure sparking in damp air. |
| **Stock** | *Gambir* Wood | Lightweight and resistant to wood-boring insects. |
| **Flash Pan** | Polished Brass | Concave design to retain gunpowder during vertical firing. |

## 2. Gunpowder Synthesis: The Tribal Recipe
The production of gunpowder (*Silai-damdawi*) was a sophisticated chemical process managed by specialized clan elders.

*   **Saltpeter (Potassium Nitrate)**: Extracted from the floors of **[[Mizo_Caves_Pukzing_MiluPuk|cave floors]]** or by boiling specific soils mixed with organic ash.
*   **Charcoal**: Created exclusively from **[[Bamboo_Ecology|Bamboo]]** or *Albizia* wood for its low ash content and high combustion speed.
*   **Sulfur**: Traded via the **[[Zokhawthar_Border_Trade_Status_2026|border trade routes]]** from volcanic regions in Burma.
*   **Ratio**: Mizo powder traditionally used a **7:2:1** ratio (Saltpeter:Charcoal:Sulfur), slightly more carbon-heavy than standard black powder to optimize for the shorter barrels of mountain rifles.

## 3. Metallurgical Balance of the "Chem" (Machete)
The **[[Mizo_Life_and_Tradition|Chem]]** is not just a knife but a precision-balanced engineering tool.
- **Center of Gravity**: Positioned roughly **60%** from the hilt to allow the weight of the blade to do the work during "Jhum Clearing."
- **Differential Tempering**: The edge is hardened for sharpness, while the spine remains flexible to absorb the shock of hitting dense bamboo fibers.

## 4. The "Zen-Bawm" (Gunpowder Flask)
- Made from buffalo horn, these flasks were airtight and waterproof. 
- Integrated a "Preservation Data": Small pieces of dry **[[Birdseye_Chili_GI_Tag]]** were often kept inside to absorb ambient moisture from the gunpowder.
