# Mizoram Economy Overview



Mizoram has one of the smallest state economies in India, but has been growing steadily through a combination of central government transfers, horticulture, and a services sector driven by the state's exceptional [[Mizoram_Literacy_and_Education|literacy rate]].

## Key Sectors

**Agriculture (28% of GSDP)**
- [[Jhum_Cultivation]] remains widespread but is in steady decline.
- **Horticulture** is the growth sector: ginger, turmeric, passion fruit, and bananas.
- **Sericulture** (silk production from mulberry and muga silkworms) is a government-promoted cottage industry.

**Trade (20% of GSDP)**
- The [[Champhai_District|Zokhawthar]] border crossing with Myanmar drives significant wholesale trade.
- Consumer goods from Southeast Asia flow inward; Indian goods flow outward.

**Services and Government (52%)**
- The largest employment sector by far — government jobs, education, healthcare, and military service are the primary careers of educated Mizo youth.

## Challenges
- No heavy industry.
- Heavy dependence on central government grants (*60–70% of state budget*).
- Geographic isolation increases logistics costs.
