# The Mizo Diaspora in the United Kingdom



The **Mizo Diaspora in the United Kingdom** has a deep historical connection, tracing back to the colonial era and the early Mizo missionaries who studied in Wales and London.

## The Welsh Connection
Because the primary missionary influence in Mizoram was from the Welsh Presbyterian Church, many early Mizo migrants settled in Wales. This has fostered long-standing academic and theological exchanges. Choirs from Mizoram often travel to the UK to participate in festivals, showcasing the **[[Mizo_Choir_Uniforms_Culture]]** and the **[[Impact_of_Gospel_on_Mizo_Pop]]**.

## Professional Communities
In the modern era, the Mizo community in the UK is largely composed of healthcare professionals, engineers, and academics. They have formed the "Mizo Association UK," which acts as a support network for new arrivals and a voice for Mizo interests. They play a significant role in promoting **[[Mizo_Music_Digital_Distribution]]** in Europe.

## Archives and Research
Many members of the UK diaspora assist researchers in navigating the **[[Mizoram_Archives_Indo-Burma_Files]]** that are housed in the British Library. Their work is essential for scholars like **[[B_Lalthangliana_Ancient_History]]** who seek to document the interaction between the **[[Sailo_Chiefs]]** and the British Crown.
