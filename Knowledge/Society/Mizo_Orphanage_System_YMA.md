# The Mizo Orphanage System and the YMA



Mizoram is unique in its "communal childcare" model, where orphanages are often managed or heavily supported by local civil society organizations, primarily the **[[Young_Mizo_Association_YMA]]**.

## The Thutak Nunpuitu Team (TNT)
The TNT, located at the outskirts of Aizawl, is the most famous example of a self-sustaining orphanage and de-addiction center. It operates on the principle of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**, where the community provides labor and resources rather than relying solely on government grants. This model is studied by NGOs globally as a benchmark for community-led social welfare.

## Integration with Modern Policy
Under the **[[Mizoram_Budget_2025_26_Analysis]]**, the state has increased its partnership with these organizations to provide vocational training for orphaned youth through the **[[Mizoram_Youth_Commission_MYC]]**. The goal is to ensure that every child is integrated into the "Kalphung Thar" (New System) of economic self-sufficiency.

## Social DNA
The success of this system is rooted in the ancient **[[Zawlbuk]]** tradition of shared responsibility. Even in the modern **[[Aizawl_District]]**, it is common for families to "adopt" an orphanage for a day, providing a feast or service, ensuring that marginalized children remain connected to the broader Mizo family.
