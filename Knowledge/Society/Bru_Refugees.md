# The Bru (Reang) Refugee Crisis

**Tags:** #society #politics #displacement

The **Bru** (also known as Reang) are an indigenous tribal group from northern Mizoram who were displaced during ethnic violence in 1997. Following riots that erupted after a Bru militant group carried out an attack on Mizo nationalists, the Bru community — approximately 35,000 people — fled across the border into relief camps in Tripura.

## The Long Exile (1997–2020)
For over two decades, the Bru refugees lived in makeshift camps in Tripura under extreme hardship, resisting resettlement in Mizoram due to fears of safety.

## The 2020 Settlement
A quadripartite agreement signed in **January 2020** between the Mizoram government, the Tripura government, the Central government, and Bru authorities allowed approximately **6,959 Bru families** (nearly 35,000 persons) to permanently settle in Tripura with land and housing grants.

This resolved one of Northeast India's longest-running refugee crises.
