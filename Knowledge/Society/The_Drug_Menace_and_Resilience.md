# The Drug Menace: History and Social Resilience



Mizoram's proximity to the "Golden Triangle" (the border regions of Myanmar, Laos, and Thailand) has historically made it a transit point and victim of clandestine narcotics trade, particularly heroin and synthetic drugs.

## Historical Context: The 1980s Peak
The drug problem peaked in the late 1980s and early 90s, coinciding with the transition period after the **[[Mizo_Peace_Accord_1986]]**. The loss of traditional social controls like the **[[Zawlbuk]]** created a void that contributed to the rise of addiction among the youth.

## The YMA Response: A Unique Social War
The fight against drugs in Mizoram is led not just by the police, but by civil society. The **[[Young_Mizo_Association_YMA]]** operates the "Supply Reduction Service (SRS)," a volunteer force that assists the **[[Mizoram_Police_Commandos]]** in identifying traffickers. This is a modern application of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]**—risking personal safety for the health of the community.

## MSACS and Healthcare
The **Mizoram State AIDS Control Society (MSACS)** has been a global leader in "Harm Reduction" strategies. Despite high prevalence rates due to intravenous drug use, the state’s community-led testing and antiretroviral therapy (ART) centers have set benchmarks for public health. This work is a primary goal of the **[[Mizoram_Socio-Economic_Development_Policy_SEDP]]** health wing.
