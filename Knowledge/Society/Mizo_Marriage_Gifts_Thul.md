# Mizo Marriage Customs: The Thul and Man-hlanchhiah

**Tags:** #society #tradition #marriage

Traditional Mizo marriage is a communal contract that involves a sophisticated exchange of gifts and symbolic payments, deeply rooted in clan solidarity.

## The Thul (Traditional Basket)
The **Thul** is a specialized, finely woven basket presented by the bride's family. It traditionally contains her finest **[[Mizo_Handicrafts_Textiles]]** (Puans) and heirloom jewelry. The quality of the weaving in the *Thul* is a matter of great pride and is often cited in **[[Mizo_Oral_Tradition]]** as a sign of a woman's industriousness and character.

## Man-hlanchhiah (The Bride Price)
Unlike a dowry, the *Man-hlanchhiah* is a token payment given by the groom's family to the bride's family. This is not seen as a "purchase" but as a "recognition of value" and a way to bind the two clans together. Even in modern ceremonies in the **[[Airlifted_Churches_of_Aizawl]]**, these traditional payments are still formally calculated and publicly announced.

## Social Role of the Palai
The entire negotiation is handled by intermediaries called *Palai*. Their role is to ensure that the marriage begins with mutual respect and that the spirit of **[[Tlawmngaihna]]** is maintained between the families. This system has contributed to the low divorce rates and high social stability in **[[Mizoram_Political_History]]**.
