# Literacy and Education in Mizoram



Mizoram is one of the most literate states in India, with a **literacy rate of approximately 91%** (2011 census) — third highest in the country after Kerala and Lakshadweep.

## Historical Roots
The [[Missionary_Arrival|Christian missionaries]] who arrived in 1894 immediately established schools alongside churches, insisting that converts learn to read the Bible in the newly created [[Mizo_Alphabet]]. Literacy and faith became inseparably linked.

## Modern Education
- The **Central Board of Secondary Education (CBSE)** operates alongside the Mizoram Board of School Education.
- **Mizoram University** (a Central University) in Aizawl offers postgraduate and doctoral programmes.
- **National Law University Mizoram**.
- The state has an extensive network of mission schools maintained by Presbyterian, Baptist, and Catholic organisations.
