# Mizo Customary Law Specifics

**Tags:** #society #law #culture

The **Mizo Customary Law** is a complex system of oral traditions that remains legally binding under **[[Article_371G]]** of the Constitution.

## Key Concepts
- **Fathang:** Traditional land rent paid to the chief (now largely historical but preserved in law).
- **Sachhiah:** The portion of a hunted animal that must be given to the village chief.
- **Sathang:** The meat portion shared with neighbours — a vital part of community bonding.
- **Man (Bride Price):** The specific rules for marriage payments.

## Modern Application
Village Councils and District Courts still invoke these laws for matters of inheritance, marriage, and land use within Mizo society.
