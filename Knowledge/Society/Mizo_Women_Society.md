# The Role of Mizo Women



Mizo women have historically occupied a more active and dignified social role than women in many other South and Southeast Asian societies.

## Traditional Role
- Women were the primary weavers of [[Puan|Puan textiles]] — a skilled craft with commercial and ceremonial value.
- Women participated fully in [[Jhum_Cultivation|Jhum farming]] — sowing, weeding, and harvesting — as equal partners.
- The communal kitchen during festivals and funerals is entirely organised and run by women.

## Customary Law Gaps
Despite their economic importance, traditional [[Mizo_Customary_Law|Mizo customary law]] disadvantaged women in:
- **Property inheritance:** Traditionally fully patrilineal — daughters received nothing from parental property.
- **Divorce:** Women's ability to initiate divorce was constrained by the [[Man_Bride_Price|Man (bride price)]] return obligations.

## Modern Transformation
Today Mizo women are among the **most educated, economically active, and publicly visible** women in any of India's tribal communities:
- [[Lalremsiami]] — India's top women's hockey player.
- High representation in nursing, teaching, civil services, and the Church.
