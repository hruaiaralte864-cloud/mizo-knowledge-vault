# The Drug Epidemic in Mizoram

**Tags:** #society #health #crisis

Mizoram has faced one of northeastern India's most severe drug abuse epidemics since the late 1980s, closely linked to the state's geographic position.

## The Golden Triangle Connection
Mizoram's eastern border with Myanmar places it adjacent to the **Golden Triangle** — the world's largest opium-producing region. Cheap heroin from the Shan State and Kachin State of Myanmar flows through the [[Champhai_District|Champhai]] border into Mizoram.

## Scale of Crisis
At its peak in the 1990s and 2000s, estimates suggested over **10% of the state's population** was affected by heroin dependency — one of the highest rates in the world at the state level. This devastated communities, caused a generation of early deaths, and triggered a catastrophic **HIV/AIDS epidemic** spread through shared needles.

## Response
- The **[[Young_Mizo_Association]]** launched anti-drug campaigns and community rehabilitation programmes.
- The church networks organised prayer-based rehabilitation.
- Government-funded **De-Addiction Centres** operate across all districts.
- The state has seen significant improvement since 2010, though the problem remains active.
